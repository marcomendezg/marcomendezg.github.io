Capillary geometry and prescribed curvature

  • Prescribed (L_{p}) curvature problem for convex capillary hypersurface by Xinqun Mei, Guofang Wang, Liangjun Weng
  • The capillary Christoffel-Minkowski problem by Xinqun Mei, Guofang Wang, Liangjun Weng
  • Capillary (L_p)-Christoffel-Minkowski problem by Yingxiang Hu, Mohammad N. Ivaki

Commentary: These works develop the capillary analogs of the Christoffel–Minkowski and (L_p)-Christoffel–Minkowski problems for convex bodies in the half-space, reducing to Hessian (quotient) equations with Robin boundary data on spherical caps. They deliver existence/uniqueness and key a priori estimates (e.g., non-collapsing), offering tools parallel to classical curvature measure problems but tailored to capillary contact-angle geometry—directly relevant to capillary surfaces, curvature flows with boundary, and variational formulations with boundary terms.

Minimal varifolds, homological filling, and isoperimetry

  • Homological Filling and Minimal Varifolds in Four-Dimensional Einstein Manifolds by Wenjie Fu, Zhifei Zhu
  • On the connectedness of a minimizing cluster's boundary by Emanuel Milman, Joe Neeman

Commentary: Fu–Zhu bound the least area of stationary integral 2-varifolds in Einstein 4-manifolds uniformly in geometric bounds via homological filling, tying quantitative filling to the existence/size of minimal surfaces. Milman–Neeman prove boundary connectedness of isoperimetric minimizers on simply connected homogeneous manifolds with at most one end, sharpening structural understanding of minimizing clusters—both feed into GMT techniques (monotonicity, (\varepsilon)-regularity, filling inequalities) central to min–max and isoperimetry.

Mean curvature flows and inverse mean curvature flow

  • Proper solutions of the (1/H)-flow and the Green kernel of the (p)-Laplacian by Luca Benatti, Luciano Mari, Marco Rigoli, Alberto G. Setti, Kai Xu
  • Parabolic free boundary phase transition and mean curvature flow by Jingeon An, Kiichi Tashiro

Commentary: Benatti et al. construct weak inverse mean curvature flows issuing from a point under curvature/isoperimetric controls via new decay bounds for the (p)-Laplacian Green kernel and relate (p)-capacitary potentials to IMCF with obstacles. An–Tashiro quantify the Allen–Cahn-to-MCF bridge for a parabolic free boundary model, proving uniform-in-time convergence; they derive the level-set evolution [ v=-H-\partial_\nu\log |\nabla u|+\frac{f(u)}{|\nabla u|} ] for (\partial_t u=\Delta u-f(u)), clarifying forcing terms that vanish in the sharp-interface limit—key for phase-field approximations of geometric flows.

Allen–Cahn/Cahn–Hilliard: traveling fronts and degenerate mobilities

  • Existence, scaling, and spectral gap for traveling fronts in the 2D renormalized Allen--Cahn equation by Gideon Chiusole, Christian Kuehn
  • On a relaxed Cahn-Hilliard tumour growth model with single-well potential and degenerate mobility by Cecilia Cavaterra, Matteo Fornoni, Maurizio Grasselli, Benoît Perthame

Commentary: Chiusole–Kuehn construct monotone traveling fronts for the renormalized 2D Allen–Cahn and establish a spectral gap in weighted spaces as the renormalization parameter vanishes—useful for stability and sharp-interface scaling. Cavaterra et al. establish existence for a relaxed Cahn–Hilliard system with degenerate mobility and singular potential and pass ( \delta\to 0 ), aligning with phase-field methods used to approximate interfacial motions and energy-dissipation structures.

Variational potential theory and rigidity for semilinear equations

  • Variational solutions of the Dirichlet problem, Lebesgue's cusp and non-local properties by Wolfgang Arendt, Daniel Daners, Manfred Sauter
  • Rigidity of weighted manifolds via classification results for semilinear equations by Giulio Ciraolo, Alberto Farina, Troy Petitt

Commentary: Arendt–Daners–Sauter identify Perron and variational solutions for continuous boundary data and analyze nonlocal boundary effects (e.g., Lebesgue’s cusp), clarifying fine boundary regularity for energy minimizers (u) with finite (\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2). Ciraolo–Farina–Petitt classify positive solutions at the Sobolev-critical exponent on weighted manifolds with nonnegative Bakry–Émery curvature, deriving rigidity of the geometry/weight—tools and Liouville-type mechanisms that often underpin uniqueness and compactness in geometric PDE.

Quantitative stratification and regularity in geometric PDE

  • Quantitative stratification and optimal regularity for harmonic almost complex structures by Chang-Yu Guo, Ming-Lun Liu, Chang-Lin Xiang
  • Regularity for fully nonlinear elliptic equations in generalized Orlicz spaces by Sun-Sig Byun, Jeongmin Han, Mikyoung Lee

Commentary: Extends Naber–Valtorta quantitative stratification to harmonic almost complex structures, proving rectifiability and optimal regularity, and establishes global Calderón–Zygmund estimates for fully nonlinear equations in generalized Orlicz spaces. These techniques echo those used in GMT for singular set structure and sharp integrability.
