High Interest Papers

Mean Curvature Flow and Singularities

  • Decay order bound for mean curvature flow near compact singularities by Sourav Ghosh

This gives a uniform bound on the decay order for the rescaled flow near multiplicity-one compact singularities of MCF, yielding unique continuation. It informs the dynamics near self-shrinkers and could interact with entropy, spectral gap, and Łojasiewicz–Simon-type estimates for MCF singularities, relevant to the analysis of blow-ups and stability of minimal/CMC models arising as critical points of (\mathcal{A}) or Gaussian area.

Minimal Surfaces: Singularities, Isotopies, and Holomorphic Constructions

  • Removing singularities of minimal surfaces by isotopies by Antonio Alarcon, Franc Forstneric
  • Exact holomorphic solution of the Hodge-dual minimal surface: a confining quantum QCD string by Alexander Migdal

Alarcón–Forstnerič show that branch points and finite-total-curvature ends can be removed by isotopies within the class of conformal minimal immersions (M\to \mathbb{R}^n), sharpening control over singularities in the complex-analytic category; this has conceptual ties to desingularization ideas in min–max. Migdal proposes an exact holomorphic “Hodge-dual” minimal surface with an area formula via Douglas–Gram matrices; while physics-driven, any rigorous new identities for areas of holomorphic/quaternionic minimal surfaces could influence variational computations of (\mathcal{A}(\Sigma)=\int_\Sigma 1\, d\mathcal{H}^2).

Geometric Flows of Higher Order

  • An analytic study of bi-harmonic flow with a forcing term by Mohammad Javad Habibi Vosta Kolaei

Analyzes a fourth-order curve flow with forcing, proving global existence, convexity preservation, and convergence under conditions, with a Monge–Ampère-type structure in a scalar reformulation. This complements Willmore/elastic flows and informs high-order curvature-gradient dynamics relevant to bending-energy functionals.

Phase-Transition Models and Computation

  • Solving Newell-Whitehead-Segel and Allen-Cahn Equations Employing Physics-Informed Neural Networks: A Comparative Analysis with Spline Methods by Ali Haider Shah, Naveed R. Butt, Asif Ahmad, Muhammad Omer Bin Saeed

Benchmarks PINNs against spline schemes for Allen–Cahn/NWS. Since Allen–Cahn (\varepsilon\to 0) approximates minimal hypersurfaces via the energy (E_\varepsilon(u)=\int \tfrac{\varepsilon}{2}|\nabla u|^2+\tfrac{1}{\varepsilon}W(u)), improved numerics can aid simulations of min–max interfaces and gradient flows for phase transitions.

Information-Theoretic Methods for Curvature and Isoperimetry

  • An Information-Theoretic Reconstruction of Curvature by Amandip Sangha
  • An Information-Theoretic Route to Isoperimetric Inequalities via Heat Flow and Entropy Dissipation by Amandip Sangha

These papers tie diffusion/entropy to geometry: (i) small-time directional relative entropy recovers the full curvature operator from heat flow without Jacobi fields; (ii) decay of mutual information under heat flow yields sharp Euclidean isoperimetry and Lévy–Gromov under curvature–dimension, with quantitative stability. This provides analytic tools parallel to Bakry–Émery and could feed into heat-flow proofs of perimeter bounds (P(E)\ge n\omega_n^{1/n}|E|^{(n-1)/n}) and stability relevant to GMT and MCF barriers.

Overdetermined Problems and Isoperimetry in Curved Spaces

  • Non-isoparametric Serrin domains of (\mathbb{S}^3) with connected toric boundary by Andrea Bisterzo, Shigeru Sakaguchi

Using Crandall–Rabinowitz bifurcation, the authors construct Serrin domains in (\mathbb{S}^3) whose boundaries are connected tori, not geodesic spheres or Clifford tori. Overdetermined torsion problems are intertwined with CMC/isoperimetric geometry and capillarity; this shows curvature permits non-rigid phenomena and enriches the landscape of potential CMC-like boundaries.

Geometric Measure Theory: Curvature Integrals and Stability

  • Continuity of total curvatures of Riemannian hypersurfaces by Mohammad Ghomi

Proves continuity of total generalized mean curvatures for hypersurfaces with positive reach (and convex bodies in Cartan–Hadamard spaces) under Hausdorff convergence. This supports stability of integral curvature functionals (\int_\Sigma H_k\, d\mathcal{H}^{n-1}) under geometric approximation, useful for varifold/finite-element limits in min–max and for compactness arguments.

Flexibility and Convex Integration in Isometric Immersion

  • Full flexibility of isometric immersions of metrics with low Hölder regularity in Poznyak theorem's dimension by Marta Lewicka

Achieves (\mathcal{C}^{1,1-}) isometric immersions into (\mathbb{R}^4) for (g\in\mathcal{C}^2) via convex integration, reaching “full flexibility.” While not directly about minimal surfaces, the flexibility/rigidity dichotomy resonates with microstructure phenomena in variational problems and informs expectations for low-regularity behavior of geometric PDE constraints.

Fractional Isoperimetry and Stability

  • A strong quantitative form of the fractional isoperimetric inequality by Eleonora Cinti, Enzo Maria Merlino, Berardo Ruffini

Strengthens quantitative fractional isoperimetry by controlling both Fraenkel asymmetry and boundary oscillations, with stability for fractional Cheeger. Nonlocal perimeters connect to phase-field models and can inform stability/rigidity for nonlocal minimal surfaces (\partial_s E) minimizing (\operatorname{Per}_s), adjacent to the researcher’s isoperimetric interests.
