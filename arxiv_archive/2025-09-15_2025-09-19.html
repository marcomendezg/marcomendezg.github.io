High Interest Papers

Uniqueness and stability in the Plateau problem near singular cones

Rigidity result for minimal hypersurfaces spanning boundaries close to the link of a minimizing quadratic cone. This clarifies when the Plateau problem admits a unique solution in a highly singular regime, informing stability of solutions near cone singularities and potentially aiding perturbative/gluing analyses around conical limits. * Uniqueness in the Plateau problem near Quadratic cones by Vishnu Nandakumaran, Gábor Székelyhidi

Nonlocal perimeters and the ( s\to 1^- ) limit toward anisotropic perimeter

Establishes ( \Gamma(L^1_{\mathrm{loc}}) )-convergence of suitably rescaled anisotropic fractional perimeters to the corresponding anisotropic local perimeter as ( s\to 1^- ), in ( \mathbb{R}^n ) and for bounded Lipschitz domains. This provides a robust bridge between nonlocal minimal surfaces and classical anisotropic minimal surfaces, with consequences for phase-field approximations, nonlocal-to-local limits, and computational schemes for anisotropic energies: [ (1-s)\,\mathrm{Per}{s,k_s}(E,\Omega)\ \xrightarrow{\ \Gamma\ }\ c_k\,\mathrm{Per}{\phi}(E,\Omega). ] * Gamma-convergence as ( s\to1^- ) of anisotropic nonlocal fractional perimeter functionals by Alberto Fanizza

CR/Engel structures in ( \mathbb{C}^3 )

Constructs immersions/embeddings of ( \mathbb{S}^1 )-bundles over closed ( 3 )-manifolds into ( \mathbb{C}^3 ) whose complex tangencies define Engel structures, yielding infinitely many non-isotopic examples in the trivial-bundle case. While not directly about minimal surfaces or variational problems, it contributes to the geometric toolkit on ( 3 )-manifolds and CR/Engel structures that occasionally interact with boundary/contact-type conditions. * Engel CR submanifolds of ( \mathbb{C}^3 ) by Eduardo Fernández, Álvaro del Pino, Wei Zhou

Geometry of neural networks and geodesics

Riemannian description of geodesics in deep linear networks, including explicit solutions in special cases and characterization of horizontal straight lines under Riemannian submersion. Relevant to the researcher’s secondary interest in the geometry of learning, gradient flows, and optimization landscapes. * Geodesics in the Deep Linear Network by Alan Chen

Regularity for nonuniformly elliptic variational problems

A survey of recent advances in regularity for nonuniformly elliptic problems. Techniques and intrinsic scaling heuristics here can inform anisotropic or inhomogeneous settings that arise in variational models adjacent to minimal surface theory and free boundary problems. * Nonuniform ellipticity in variational problems and regularity by Cristiana De Filippis, Giuseppe Mingione

Critical Schrödinger-type problems under mass constraint

Existence of two positive normalized solutions (ground state and mountain pass) for a quasilinear Schrödinger equation with critical nonlinearity on bounded star-shaped domains. Methodologically relevant via constrained variational principles on ( L^2 )-spheres and dual approaches. * Normalized solutions of quasilinear Schrödinger equation with Sobolev critical exponent on star-shaped bounded domains by Ru Yan

TDA in Finsler geometry

Extends Vietoris–Rips and Čech constructions to Finsler settings with directional dependence, with links to information-theoretic TDA. Aligns with the computational geometry/TDA secondary interest, suggesting new geometric priors for data analysis. * Topological Data Analysis in Finsler Spaces by Rafael Cavalcanti, Nelson Leal, Danilo Barros de Souza, Serafim Rodrigues, Mathieu Desroches

Free-boundary limits in long-range segregation models

Studies a fully nonlinear system (negative Pucci diffusion) depending on a small parameter ( \varepsilon ), proving existence and convergence as ( \varepsilon\to0^+ ) to a segregated free-boundary configuration with positive separation. The limiting supports have finite perimeter and semi-convexity, echoing geometric-measure-theoretic features familiar from minimal surface/free boundary theory. * A nonlinear model for long-range segregation by Howen Chuah, Stefania Patrizi, Monica Torres

Anisotropic integral representation in the calculus of variations

A concise review of integral representation results for local functionals driven by Lipschitz anisotropies.
