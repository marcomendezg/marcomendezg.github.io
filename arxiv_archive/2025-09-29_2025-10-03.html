Mean Curvature Flow: Regularity, Singularities, and Ancient Solutions

  • The epsilon-regularity theorem for Brakke flows near triple junctions by Salvatore Stuvard, Yoshihiro Tonegawa
  • Approximate mean curvature flows of a general varifold, and their limit spacetime Brakke flow by Blanche Buet, Gian Paolo Leonardi, Simon Masnou, Abdelmouksit Sagueni
  • Mean curvature flow through singularities by Robert Haslhofer
  • An Ancient Stacked Pancake Solution to Mean Curvature Flow by Mat Langford, Alexander Mramor, Louis Yudowitz
  • First eigenvalue and nodal domains of the drift Laplacian on symmetric self-shrinkers in $\mathbb{R}^3$ by Elham Matinpour

Commentary: These papers push the analytic and structural theory of mean curvature flow (MCF). Stuvard–Tonegawa prove an (\varepsilon)-regularity theorem for Brakke flows at static multiplicity-one triple junctions, a parabolic counterpart to L. Simon’s stationary theory and highly relevant for varifold flows arising via Ilmanen’s elliptic regularization. Buet–Leonardi–Masnou–Sagueni propose a general approximation scheme for MCF starting from arbitrary varifolds and prove convergence to spacetime Brakke flows under rectifiability. Haslhofer’s survey synthesizes the emerging “flow through singularities” theory (notably in (\mathbb{R}^3)) and reports a classification of all noncollapsed singularities in (\mathbb{R}^4), outlining sharp open problems. Langford–Mramor–Yudowitz construct a new embedded ancient nonconvex solution (“stacked pancakes”), enriching the catalog of ancient models for singularity formation. Matinpour shows (\lambda_1=\tfrac12) for the drift Laplacian on highly symmetric self-shrinkers, indicating a universal spectral feature tied to symmetry classes and potentially constraining instability modes.

Minimal Surfaces: Plateau, Free Boundary, and Conformal Geometry

  • Uniqueness in the Plateau problem for calibrated currents by Bryan Dimler, Chen-Kuan Lee
  • The Y-Cone type Free Boundary Minimal Surfaces in the unit ball by Elham Matinpour
  • Rigidity of Minimal Surfaces with Y-singularities and low Morse Index in $\mathbb{R}^3$ by Elham Matinpour
  • Minimally embedded Riemann surfaces in $\mb{S}^3$ and the conformal deformation of their metrics by Santiago R. Simanca

Commentary: Dimler–Lee give a strong uniqueness theorem for calibrated integral currents with connected (C^{3,\alpha}) boundary, leveraging boundary regularity and unique continuation for the minimal surface system—directly impacting Plateau-type problems in calibrated settings. Matinpour’s two notes establish rigidity for Y-singular free boundary minimizers in (B^3) and classify low Morse index Y-singular minimal surfaces in (\mathbb{R}^3), clarifying the landscape of singular minimizers and their stability. Simanca proves that area-preserving conformal deformations of metrics on minimally embedded surfaces in (\mathbb{S}^3) can be realized by ambient conformal diffeomorphisms preserving minimality, with applications to Lawson surfaces—linking minimal embeddings, Willmore energy, and conformal geometry.

Variational Models and Quantitative GMT

  • Finite number of traces for Mumford-Shah minimizers in dimension 2 by Camille Labourie, Antoine Lemenant
  • Quantitative regularity for minimizing intrinsic fractional harmonic maps by Y. -Y. Wang, C. -L. Xiang, G. -F. Zheng

Commentary: Labourie–Lemenant resolve a question of De Giorgi by proving that 2D Mumford–Shah minimizers have at most three limit traces near the crack set, sharpening fine-structure understanding of singular sets in free-discontinuity problems. Wang–Xiang–Zheng develop quantitative stratification for minimizing intrinsic fractional harmonic maps (à la Moser–Roberts), yielding improved singular set dimension bounds and global regularity estimates—tools and themes parallel to those in minimal surface regularity.

Overdetermined Problems and Symmetry

  • On the Serrin's problem with Robin boundary conditions by Nunzia Gavitone, Riccardo Molinarolo

Commentary: Extends Serrin-type rigidity to the Robin case for the torsion problem, under structural assumptions on the domain and parameter. Overdetermined PDE symmetry results remain closely tied to geometric variational structures and isoperimetric heuristics, with potential cross-talk to capillarity and free boundary problems.

Convex/Capillary Geometry and Isoperimetry

  • The $L_p$ dual Minkowski problem for capillary hypersurfaces by Ya Gao
  • The weighted isoperimetric inequality and Sobolev inequality outside convex sets by Lu Chen, Jiali Lan

Commentary: Gao solves an (L_p) dual Minkowski problem for capillary hypersurfaces via a Monge–Ampère equation with Robin boundary data, relevant to capillary surface geometry. Chen–Lan prove sharp weighted capillary isoperimetric and Sobolev inequalities outside convex sets using a (\lambda_w)-ABP method, enabling symmetrization techniques with capillary boundary terms.

Calculus of Variations and Nonlinear/Nonlocal PDE

  • A note on the recovery sequence in the double gradient model for phase transitions by Jakob Deutsch
