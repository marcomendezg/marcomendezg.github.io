Phase-Field, Allen–Cahn, and Ginzburg–Landau

  • Well-posedness and sharp interface limit of a non-isothermal Navier--Stokes/Allen--Cahn model by Helmut Abels, Alice Marveggio, Andrea Poiatti
  • Ginzburg-Landau minimizers with high topological degrees in an annulus by Amandine Aftalion, Rémy Rodiac
  • Identification of Source Terms in the Ginzburg-Landau Equation from Final Data by Roberto Morales, Javier-Ramírez-Ganga
  • Vortex Solutions for A Mixed Boundary-Value Problem in the Abelian-Higgs Model with A Neutral Scalar Field by Guange Su, Xiaosen Han
  • Global well-posedness of strong solutions to a bulk-surface Navier-Stokes-Cahn-Hilliard model with non-degenerate mobilities in two dimensions by Jonas Stange

Commentary: Strong well-posedness and a sharp-interface limit toward Navier–Stokes/mean curvature flow connect directly to Allen–Cahn approximations of interfaces. High-degree GL on an annulus identifies a critical degree (O(|\ln \varepsilon|)) separating giant-vortex vs. mixed regimes, sharpening our understanding of vortex concentration near boundaries. The inverse GL source-identification problem aligns with computational/variational interests (adjoint gradients, stability), while Abelian–Higgs vortices with a neutral field provide rigorous phase diagrams for topological defects. The bulk-surface NS–CH result treats realistic coupling of interfacial dynamics on boundaries with non-degenerate mobility.

Geometric Measure Theory: Currents and Cycles

  • On smooth approximation of integral cycles mod 2 by Gianmarco Caldini
  • A PDE perspective on the flat chain conjecture by Andrea Marchese

Commentary: Caldini proves flat-norm approximation of mod 2 integral cycles by almost-smooth representatives with singular set of codimension (3), the unoriented analogue crucial for min–max in mod 2 homology. Marchese’s survey ties the flat chain conjecture to a Lipschitz estimate for a PDE, suggesting analytic routes to bridge metric currents and flat chains that could impact varifold/chain compactness tools.

Free Boundary Problems and Singularity Analysis

  • On the asymptotic properties of solutions to one-phase free boundary problems by Max Engelstein, Daniel Restrepo, Zihui Zhao

Commentary: Uniqueness of blow-ups (à la Allard–Almgren) and blow-down rigidity (à la Simon–Solomon) for one-phase Bernoulli solutions modeled on one-homogeneous singular profiles sharpen the singularity picture for non-minimizing free boundaries, paralleling techniques from minimal surface regularity.

Prescribed Mean Curvature and CMC Surfaces

  • Pairs of Embedded Spheres with Pinched Prescribed Mean Curvature by Liam Mazurowski, Xin Zhou

Commentary: Existence of at least two embedded (S^2\subset S^3) with prescribed mean curvature (h) under a pinching (h<h_0\approx 0.547) (and sign-changing variants) indicates a multiplicity phenomenon for PMC spheres, resonant with min–max and variational constructions in the CMC/PMC landscape.

Gradient Flows of Geometric Energies

  • Total variation flow of curves in Riemannian manifolds by Lorenzo Giacomelli, Michał Łasica, Salvador Moll

Commentary: An (L^2)-gradient flow for the manifold-valued total variation of curves is shown to have global strong solutions (BV data), uniqueness under (\mathrm{sec}\le 0), and finite-time extinction to constants—bridging BV flows and geometric curve flows with variational structure.

Special Lagrangian and Fully Nonlinear Estimates

  • Doubling Argument of the Hessian Estimate for the Special Lagrangian Equation on General Phases with Constraints by Cheuk Yan Fung

Commentary: A new doubling method yields Hessian bounds for the special Lagrangian equation under general phase constraints, bypassing the Michael–Simon inequality and supplying Alexandrov-type theorems—useful for calibrated geometry and minimal graph regularity.

Minimal/CMC Surfaces in Homogeneous Spaces

  • Helix surfaces in Lorentzian Heisenberg group by Lorenzo Pellegrino

Commentary: Complete classification and explicit parametrizations of minimal and CMC helix surfaces (constant-angle) in Lorentzian Heisenberg groups enrich the catalogue of explicit examples in homogeneous 3-manifolds, informing comparison and barrier constructions.

Biharmonic Functions on Open Manifolds

  • The qualitative behavior for biharmonic functions on open manifolds by Lin Wang, Miaomiao Zhu

Commentary: On manifolds with nonnegative Ricci curvature, bounded biharmonic functions are constant and polynomial-growth spaces are finite dimensional with Weyl-type bounds—tools that may inform fourth-order energies (e.g., Willmore) and potential-theoretic aspects of higher-order curvature functionals.
