High Interest Papers

Monotonicity and Functional Inequalities Motivated by Geometric Flows

Briefly: Connects elliptic and parabolic monotonicity via new “deficit” quantities, yielding an elliptic proof of the log-Sobolev inequality. This unifies tools underlying Huisken-type monotonicities and could inform min-max/Allen–Cahn analyses where parabolic and elliptic perspectives meet.

  • Deficit functions and the log Sobolev inequality by Tobias Holck Colding, William P. Minicozzi II

Minimal Surfaces: Constructions, Rigidity, and Stability

Briefly: New construction and classification inputs for embedded minimal surfaces and their singular limits. Highlights include a variational route to Kapouleas-style doublings via a Coulomb-type energy, and explicit stable/isolated multiplicity-(2) singularities—both relevant to min-max limits and index/degeneracy issues. The twistor/holomorphic viewpoints give structural control in codimension (2k).

  • Minimal surface doublings and electrostatics for Schrödinger operators by Adrian Chun-Pong Chu, Daniel Stern
  • Stationary and stable varifolds with singularities by Camillo De Lellis, Jonas Hirsch, Luca Spolaor
  • The twistor lifts of surfaces in 4-spaces by Naoya Ando
  • Holomorphicity of parabolic stable minimal surfaces of high codimension by Da Rong Cheng, Spiro Karigiannis, Jesse Madnick

Mean Curvature Flow and Translators

Briefly: Robust noncollapsing for fully nonlinear speeds ( \Psi \circ F ) advances maximum-principle control for general curvature flows; global “flat flow” existence for volume-preserving MCF with contact angle addresses capillary dynamics; translators with planar ends are classified (only vertical planes), sharpening the structure of eternal MCF solutions with finite topology.

  • Noncollapsing for Curvature Flows with Inhomogeneous Speeds by Weimin Sheng, Ye Zhu
  • Existence of flat flows for volume-preserving mean curvature flow with contact angle by Jiwoong Jang
  • Translators Asymptotic to Planes by Stephen Lynch, Giuseppe Tinaglia

Capillary Surfaces and Contact Angle Rigidity

Briefly: Rigidity for volume-preserving critical points of the capillary energy in the hydrophobic regime ( \theta \in (90^\circ,180^\circ) ) (up to an ( \mathcal H^n )-null tangential boundary) without regularity assumptions; anisotropic analogues under lower density bounds. Strong GMT methodology for sets of finite perimeter aligns with capillarity at fixed contact angle ( \theta ).

  • Rigidity of critical points of hydrophobic capillary functionals by Antonio De Rosa, Robin Neumayer, Reinaldo Resende

Critical Exponent Variational PDE (Brézis–Nirenberg)

Briefly: New existence theory for least-energy solutions in ( n=3 ) near Laplacian eigenvalues via a novel weighted principal-eigenvalue minimization; discontinuities at eigenvalues in ( n\ge 4 ). Complemented by a focused set of results/open problems in ( n=6 ). Techniques resonate with Yamabe/critical points and bubble analysis central to min-max and phase-transition limits.

  • Least-energy solutions of the Brézis-Nirenberg problem in the non-coercive case in dimension (3) by Hussein Cheikh Ali, Bruno Premoselli
  • On Brezis-Nirenberg problems: open questions and new results in dimension six by Fengliu Li, Giusi Vaira, Juncheng Wei, Yuanze Wu

Phase Transitions and De Giorgi-type Classification

Briefly: For a coupled two-component system in ( \mathbb R^3 ) (BEC with Rabi coupling), monotone entire solutions with opposite (x_3)-monotonicities are 1D. This is a De Giorgi-type result for multi-well/multi-component energies, relevant to interfaces modeling and to analogues of Allen–Cahn/Ginzburg–Landau.

  • Phase transitions in two-component Bose-Einstein condensates with Rabi frequency (I): The De Giorgi conjecture for the local problem in (\mathbb{R}^{3}) by Leyun Wu, Chilin Zhang

Inverse Problems for Prescribed Mean Curvature

Briefly: In 2D, the source ( H(x) ) in [ \nabla \cdot \Big( \frac{\nabla u}{\sqrt{1+|\nabla u|^2}} \Big) = H(x) ] is uniquely determined by the Dirichlet-to-Neumann map. Higher-order linearization plus decoupling via a Liouville-type uniqueness for conformal maps tackles the quasilinear anisotropy. Potential implications for recovering geometric forcing in prescribed mean curvature settings.

  • An Inverse Problem for the Prescribed Mean Curvature by Tony Liimatainen, Janne Nurminen

Medium Interest Papers

Shape Optimization and Spectral Problems

Briefly: Proves that quasi-open minimizers of first eigenvalues (polyharmonic, Lamé, Stokes) are open, removing dimension restrictions via Campanato theory. Pertinent to regularity and free-boundary structure in spectral shape optimization.

  • Openness of shape optimizers in higher-order and non-scalar problems by Rupert L. Frank

Willmore Energy and Higher-Order Geometric PDEs

Briefly: Existence/regularity for graphical Willmore with low-regularity boundary/Dirichlet data using a divergence-form rewrite and weighted Sobolev spaces; extends to Helfrich/surface diffusion. Techniques may transfer to constrained higher-order geometric variational problems.

  • Graphical Willmore Problems with Low-Regularity Boundary and Dirichlet Data by Boris Gulyak

Functional-Analytic Tools on Non-Compact Manifolds

Briefly: Extends the “extended Sobolev scale” (H^\varphi(X)) to manifolds of bounded geometry and establishes interpolation/mapping properties for PUPDOs. Useful background machinery for PDE on non-compact ambient spaces.

  • Extended Sobolev Scale on Non-Compact Manifolds by Ognjen Milatovic

Convex and Capillary Geometry

Briefly: Establishes the capillary Blaschke–Santalo inequality for unconditional, strictly convex capillary hypersurfaces when ( \theta \in (0,\tfrac{\pi}{2}) ), and shows unboundedness of the capillary volume product for ( \theta \in (\tfrac{\pi}{2},\pi) ). Connects convex inequalities with capillarity.

  • On the conjectured capillary Blaschke-Santaló inequality by Carlos Cabezas-Moreno, Yingxiang Hu, Mohammad N. Ivaki

Geometry of Neural Representations

Briefly: Argues that feature graphs in feedforward networks evolve akin to a discrete Ricci flow, clarifying how nonlinearity shapes geometry and suggesting practical diagnostics. Of conceptual interest at the interface of geometric flows and learning.

  • Neural Feature Geometry Evolves as Discrete Ricci Flow by Moritz Hehl, Max von Renesse, Melanie Weber
