Variational Constructions and Discrete Models of Minimal Surfaces

  • Constructing entire minimal graphs by evolving planes by Chung-Jun Tsai, Mao-Pei Tsui, Jingbo Wan, Mu-Tao Wang
  • Discrete minimal surfaces: Old and New by Wai Yeung Lam, Masashi Yasumoto
  • Discrete Koenigs nets, inscribed quadrics and autoconjugate curves by Niklas Christoph Affolter, Alexander Yves Fairley

Commentary: New explicit families of entire minimal graphs (via a Grassmannian geodesic ansatz) broaden the catalog of nontrivial solutions to the minimal surface system in high codimension. The discrete works illuminate structure-preserving discretizations with variational characterizations and Weierstrass-type representations—useful both for computation and for connecting discrete minimal surfaces to Teichmüller-type data.

Mean Curvature Flow: Singularities, Solitons, and Evolving Backgrounds

  • On the rate of convergence of cylindrical singularity in mean curvature flow by Yiqi Huang, Xinrui Zhao
  • Mean curvature flow into an ambient Riemannian manifold evolving by Ricci flow coupled with harmonic map heat flow by José N. V. Gomes, Matheus Hudson, Carlos M. de Sousa

Commentary: The first paper gives a rigidity/unique continuation result at the cylindrical singularity model under global small-graph assumptions and constructs counterexamples showing sharpness—important for understanding generic MCF singularities. The second derives a Huisken-type monotonicity and Harnack expressions for MCF in a coupled Ricci/harmonic-map evolving background, suggesting new monotonicity tools for flows beyond fixed ambient metrics.

Phase Transitions and Γ-limits (Allen–Cahn and Nonlocal Variants)

  • $Γ$-convergence for higher order nonlocal phase transitions by Hardy Chan, Serena Dipierro, Mattia Freguglia, Marco Inversi, Enrico Valdinoci

Commentary: For (0<s<3/4), the (\varepsilon)-rescaled sum of (s)-fractional Allen–Cahn energy and the squared (L^2)-norm of its first variation (\Gamma)-converges to either classical perimeter (for (s\ge 1/2)) or (2s)-fractional perimeter (for (s<1/2)). Notably, the expected curvature-dependent terms do not appear in this regime, refining the heuristic landscape for higher-order penalizations in phase-field approximations of minimal surfaces.

Isoperimetric and Geometric Inequalities

  • Quantitative Lorentzian isoperimetric inequalities by Christian Lange, Jonas W. Peteranderl
  • Remark on a geometric inequality for closed hypersurfaces in weighted manifolds by Adam Rudnik
  • Variational properties of the total inverse mean curvature in the plane under boundary constraints by Julián Pozuelo, Simone Verzellesi, Giacomo Vianello

Commentary: Optimal stability (Fraenkel asymmetry) in Lorentzian isoperimetry parallels Euclidean quantitative estimates and suggests refined geometric correction terms. In weighted manifolds with nonnegative Bakry–Émery Ricci, sharp inequalities and rigidity generalize Willmore-type results. The planar inverse mean curvature functional with boundary constraints shows strong stability and local minimality—an accessible model for curvature-driven variational problems.

Lagrangian and CMC Surface Geometry

  • Doubling and the two-dimensional critical valued Lagrangian phase by Arunima Bhattacharya, Ravi Shankar, Jeremy Wall
  • Hopf differentials and curvature line flows on time-like CMC surfaces by Naoya Ando, Masaaki Umehara

Commentary: For the 2D Lagrangian mean curvature equation at critical phase, a modified doubling technique yields interior Hessian/gradient bounds despite a degenerate Jacobi inequality—advancing regularity at the delicate critical regime. The second paper ties Hopf differential order to the index of curvature line flows at umbilics on time-like CMC surfaces, refining the local differential-topological picture in Lorentzian settings.

Inverse Problems via Minimal Surfaces

  • Generalized boundary rigidity and minimal surface transform by Leonard Busch, Tony Liimatainen, Mikko Salo, Leo Tzou

Commentary: Areas of embedded minimal surfaces determine conformal perturbations of analytic metrics under an ampleness condition, with Hölder stability. The “minimal surface transform” fits a double fibration framework (Bolker condition) and is invertible under foliation assumptions. This broadens the inverse geometry toolkit directly around minimal surfaces, with methods potentially transferrable to geometric tomography.

Sub-Riemannian and Non-Euclidean Surface Theory

  • Traslation surfaces in the Heisenberg Group by Christiam Figueroa

Commentary: Classification of translation surfaces with vanishing intrinsic curvature in the Heisenberg group adds to the catalog of explicitly analyzable surfaces in sub-Riemannian geometries, relevant for calibrations and comparisons with minimal-type objects in non-Euclidean settings.

Geometric PDE Blow-up Phenomena

  • Self-similar blowup from arbitrary data for supercritical wave maps with additive noise by Irfan Glogić, Martina Hofmanová, Eliseo Luongo

Commentary: For corotational supercritical wave maps ( \mathbb{R}^{1+d}\to\mathbb{S}^d ) with additive noise ((d\ge 3)), self-similar blow-up occurs with positive probability for any initial data, reinforcing the conjectured deterministic blow-up picture and illustrating how stochastic perturbations interact with geometric PDE criticality.
