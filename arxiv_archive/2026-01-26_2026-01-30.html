High Interest Papers

Mean Curvature Flow: singularities, topology change, and structure

  • Lectures on Mean Curvature Flow and Related Equations by Tom Ilmanen
  • Singularities of Mean Curvature Flow of Surfaces by Tom Ilmanen

Commentary: Ilmanen’s pair advances core questions on MCF. The lectures synthesize insights on nonuniqueness, topology change, and singularity formation. The research paper proves that first-time blow-ups of smooth embedded surfaces in (n=3) are smoothly embedded shrinkers (no branching), possibly with multiplicity; in general (n), tangent flows are smooth branched shrinkers. A new local Gauss–Bonnet identity underpins the analysis, complementing monotonicity methods and directly informing min–max/regularity strategies for MCF.

Capillary and weighted minimal surfaces: half-space theorems and singular cones

  • Half-space type theorems for a class of weighted minimal surfaces in $\mathbb{R}^{3}$ by A. L. Martínez-Triviño, J. P. dos Santos, G. Tinaglia
  • Area-minimizing capillary cones by Benjy Firester, Raphael Tsiamis, Yipeng Wang

Commentary: New half-space type results for height-weighted minimal surfaces in (\mathbb{R}^3) extend Hoffman–Meeks phenomena to weighted settings (including stochastic completeness), with barriers in cones and non-vertical half-spaces. Complementarily, the construction of area-minimizing capillary cones with bi-orthogonal symmetry yields non-flat minimizing singularities in ambient dimension (n\ge 8) (singular set of codimension (7)), sharpening capillary regularity and bridging to one-phase free boundaries via Alt–Caffarelli. Both papers intersect GMT, maximum principles, and free-boundary minimal geometry.

Nonlocal minimal surfaces and boundary regularity

  • Regularity of the trace of nonlocal minimal graphs by Serena Dipierro, Ovidiu Savin, Enrico Valdinoci

Commentary: Establishes that at “stickiness” points the boundary trace of nonlocal minimal graphs is (C^{1,\gamma}), hence boundary continuity implies boundary differentiability. This refines boundary regularity for nonlocal minimal surfaces, relevant for phase-transition approximations to minimal hypersurfaces and for nonlocal perimeter functionals.

Classical minimal surface characterizations

Commentary: A rigidity characterization via isogonal lines being generalized helices/pseudo-geodesics isolates the Enneper surface and certain helix surfaces in (\mathbb{R}^3). Connects classical differential geometry with integrable surface representations relevant to minimal-surface model geometries.

Self-similar solutions in geometric flows

  • Jellyfish exist by Ben Andrews, Glen Wheeler

Commentary: Proves the existence of infinitely many homothetic expanders for elastic flow and epicyclic shrinkers/expanders for curve diffusion and ideal flows. Rich family of self-similar solutions gives new models for singularity profiles and long-time asymptotics, paralleling shrinker/expander roles in MCF and informing variational–flow frameworks.

Free-boundary variational problems: Alt–Caffarelli and Willmore

  • Regularity of Lipschitz free boundaries for weak solutions of Alt-Caffarelli type problems by Joan Domingo-Pasarin, Xavier Ros-Oton
  • Stability of the free boundary Willmore problem by Anna Dall'Acqua, Fabian Rupp, Reiner Schätzle, Manuel Schlierf

Commentary: For Alt–Caffarelli, Lipschitz free boundaries are upgraded to (C^\infty) under smooth data, closing the gap between viscosity and weak notions. For free-boundary Willmore flow, a new Łojasiewicz–Simon inequality on infinite-dimensional manifolds yields convergence near local minimizers and quantitative stability near free-boundary minimal surfaces—techniques reminiscent of energy decay and gradient-inequality methods in curvature flows.

  • Blowup stability of wave maps without symmetry by Roland Donninger, Frederick Moscatelli
