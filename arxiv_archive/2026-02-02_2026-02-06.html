Advances in Equivariant Min-Max and the Spherical Bernstein Problem

  • Equivariant min-max theory and the spherical Bernstein problem in $\mathbb{S}^4$ by Tongrui Wang, Zhichao Wang, Xin Zhou

This develops a (G)-equivariant min-max theory (with 3D orbit space and reduced genus bounds) to produce an embedded non-equatorial minimal hypersphere in (\mathbb{S}^4), giving a new solution to Chern’s spherical Bernstein problem and confirming a Pitts–Rubinstein assertion. The tools—regularity for (G)-equivariant Plateau/isotopy minimization and symmetry-adapted sweepouts—are directly relevant to min-max constructions of minimal hypersurfaces and could be adapted to settings (e.g., hyperbolic 3-manifolds) where symmetry lowers complexity.

Phase Transitions and Allen–Cahn: Bounded Index Rigidity

  • Phase transitions with bounded index: Parallels to De Giorgi's conjecture by Enric Florit-Simon

Strong rigidity for finite Morse index Allen–Cahn solutions with bounded energy density: in (\mathbb{R}^4) they are 1D, and conditionally the same for (4\le n\le 7). On closed 4-manifolds, transition layers are smooth and behave like minimal hypersurfaces. This tightens the Allen–Cahn/minimal-surface correspondence beyond stability, sharpening the dimension (7) threshold and informing min-max/phase-transition approaches to produce and analyze minimal hypersurfaces, including index control and end-structure (planar/catenoidal) phenomena.

Area-Minimizing Surfaces in (\mathbb{R}^4): Algebraicity and Quantitative Bounds

  • Entire area-minimizing surfaces in R^4 are algebraic by Nick Edelen, Luis Atzin Franco Reyna, Paul Minter

Entire 2D area-minimizing or stable surfaces in (\mathbb{R}^4) with quadratic area growth are shown to be algebraic, cut out by finitely many holomorphic polynomials, with degrees controlled by the density at infinity; this yields bounds on singular set size and genus. This is a striking GMT classification linking asymptotics to algebraic structure, with implications for uniqueness of tangent cones, quantitative regularity, and the structure of stable/minimizing cones—highly relevant for understanding singularities and global geometry of minimal surfaces.

Mean Curvature Flow in Hyperbolic Space: Translators and Stability

  • Dynamical Stability of Translating Solitons to Mean Curvature Flow in Hyperbolic Space by Ronaldo F. de Lima, Álvaro K. Ramos

Introduces translating solitons to MCF in (\mathbb{H}^{n+1}) with (n+1\ge 3), constructing rotational “winglike” translators (barriers) and proving dynamical stability of horospheres as radial graphs via White’s avoidance and parabolic maximum principles. This extends the translator toolkit to negatively curved ambient spaces, informing stability/uniqueness questions and barrier methods for MCF in curved manifolds—useful both intrinsically and for variationally flavored flow arguments.

Medium Interest Papers

Computational Geometry and TDA for PDEs and Flows

  • Computing Diffusion Geometry by Iolo Jones, David Lanners

A data-driven framework to compute differential-geometric quantities—geodesic distances, curvature, vector calculus objects—and to solve PDEs/flows on point clouds. This could aid computational experiments for gradient flows (e.g., Allen–Cahn), approximate geodesics, and curvature-driven processes, aligning with interests in computational methods for variational problems.

Willmore Energy and Variational Geometry

  • Two classes of Willmore Surfaces in $\mathbb{S}^2\times \mathbb{S}^2$ by Xiaoling Chai, Shimpei Kobayashi, Changping Wang, Zhenxiao Xie

Classifies Willmore surfaces that are minimal in (\mathbb{S}^2\times\mathbb{S}^2) (slices/diagonals or minimal in (\mathbb{S}^2\times\mathbb{S}^1) via a 1D sinh–Gordon reduction) and characterizes product-type Willmore surfaces via elastic curves. Though not directly minimal-surface theory, the variational and integrable-structure aspects have methodological overlap with calculus of variations and could inform CMC/Willmore analogs of min-max constructions.
