Curvature Flows: Mean Curvature Flow, Curve Shortening, and Brakke Theory

  • Singularities of Curve Shortening Flow with Convex Projections by Qi Sun
  • Local umbilic, convexity and cylindrical estimates for fully nonlinear curvature flows by Mat Langford, James McCoy
  • Mean curvature flow converging to an minimizing cone and its Hardt-Simon foliation by Jiuzhou Huang
  • (L^2) normal velocity implies strong solution for graphical Brakke flows by Kotaro Motegi

Commentary: New structural and regularity inputs for curvature flows. Sun proves Type I singularities and asymptotically circular blow-ups for closed immersed curves in ( \mathbb{R}^n ) having a one-to-one convex projection, and an analogue of Huisken’s round-point conjecture via a perturbation into ( \mathbb{R}^{n+2} ). Langford–McCoy localize Huisken–Stampacchia to one-homogeneous fully nonlinear speeds, giving sharp local pinching/cylindrical estimates crucial for singularity analysis and surgery. Huang constructs MCFs converging (under different rescalings) to an area-minimizing strictly stable hypercone and its Hardt–Simon foliation, removing symmetry assumptions and strengthening the Velázquez paradigm. Motegi gives a key bridge from Brakke varifold flows with (L^2) normal velocity to strong/classical graphical solutions under mild regularity, enhancing the analytic toolbox in GMT/MCF.

Minimal Surfaces, Area-Minimizing Cones, Index, and Deformations

  • On FKM isoparametric hypersurfaces in (\mathbb{S}^n \times \mathbb{S}^n) and new area-minimizing cones by Hongbin Cui
  • Minimal Surfaces of Finite Genus: Classification, Dynamics and Laminations by Joaquín Pérez
  • Note on energy index and first eigenvalue of minimal surfaces in spheres by Matilde Gianocca
  • Neutral Deformation Modes of Minimal Surfaces by Ande M. Sonnet, Epifanio G. Virga

Commentary: Cui produces many new area-minimizing cones using a refined FKM isoparametric foliation on ( \mathbb{S}^n \times \mathbb{S}^n ) and Lawlor’s curvature criterion, including codimension-2 cones in ( \mathbb{R}^{2n+2} ) for ( n \ge 63 ), adding to the catalogue of stable singular models. Pérez’s survey synthesizes decades of work (Meeks–Pérez–Ros) on properly embedded minimal surfaces in ( \mathbb{R}^3 ), lamination techniques, dynamics, index/topology bounds, and the embedded Calabi–Yau problem—an invaluable reference with open problems. Gianocca refines the relation between the energy index and the first eigenvalue for minimal immersions in ( S^n ), giving conditions ensuring negative second variation beyond Möbius directions; this informs index/stability questions. Sonnet–Virga classify neutral stretching/drilling/bending modes taking minimal surfaces to minimal surfaces, highlighting that isometries yield globally neutral “soft” deformations, relevant for stability and elasticity perspectives.

Generalizations and Special Curvature Hypersurfaces

  • Families of surfaces with constant ratio of principal curvatures and Plateau's problem by Mikhail Skopenkov, Khusrav Yorov
  • Surfaces and Hypersurfaces with Prescribed Radial Mean Curvature by Marcelo Lopes Ferro, Armando M. V. Corro
  • On Hopf hypersurfaces of the complex hyperbolic quadric with constant principal curvatures by Haizhong Li, Hiroshi Tamaru, Zeke Yao

Commentary: Skopenkov–Yorov construct families of CRPC surfaces (constant ratio of principal curvatures) passing through a given minimal surface and obtain a partial Plateau theory, offering a controlled deformation framework beyond minimal/CMC. Ferro–Corro give local classifications under prescribed radial mean curvature and construct higher-dimensional analogues, intersecting with prescribed curvature PDE methods. Li–Tamaru–Yao classify Hopf hypersurfaces with few constant principal curvatures in a noncompact rank-one symmetric space, supplying models and rigidity phenomena for special curvature geometries.

Allen–Cahn and Phase-Transition Models

  • An Allen-Cahn tumor growth model with temperature by Stefania Gatti, Erica Ipocoana, Alain Miranville

Commentary: A non-isothermal Allen–Cahn/Ginzburg–Landau system derived via microforces; the authors establish global well-posedness. While motivated by tumor growth, the analytic structure aligns with phase-field approximations of interfaces/minimal surfaces and could inform thermally coupled Allen–Cahn limits.

Hyperbolic 3-Manifolds and Rigidity

  • Marked Poincaré rigidity near hyperbolic metrics and injectivity of the Lichnerowicz Laplacian in dimension 3 by Karen Butt, Alena Erchenko, Tristan Humbert, Thibault Lefeuvre, Amie Wilkinson

Commentary: Introduces the marked Poincaré determinant (unstable-volume growth data along closed geodesics) and proves local rigidity in dimension 3 near hyperbolic metrics; also shows injectivity of the Lichnerowicz Laplacian on TT tensors. This advances geodesic-flow-based rigidity in hyperbolic 3-manifolds, with potential implications for geometric analysis on negatively curved spaces.

Trapped Surfaces and Geometric Analysis in General Relativity

  • Semi-Global Existence and Trapped Surface Formation for the Einstein-Vlasov System by Nikolaos Athanasiou, Puskar Mondal, Shing-Tung Yau

Commentary: Large-data, symmetry-free formation of trapped surfaces in (3+1) Einstein–Vlasov via a hierarchy of curvature/kinetic estimates and refined renormalization. While in Lorentzian geometry, techniques around marginally trapped surfaces resonate with variational and curvature estimates used in minimal-surface theory.

Variational Interface Energies, Anisotropy, and Phase-Field Approximations

  • Curvature penalization of strongly anisotropic interfaces models and their phase-field approximation by Jean-François Babadjian, Blanche Buet, Michael Goldman
  • Non-spherical minimizers in the generalized liquid drop model for Yukawa and truncated Coulomb potentials by Lia Bronsard, Benoît Merlet, Marc Pegon
  • A family of interaction energy minimizers supported on two intervals by Steven B. Damelin, Ruiwen Shu

Commentary: These works explore nonconvex anisotropic surface energies regularized by Willmore-type terms, shape transitions and non-spherical minimizers under screened interactions, and new techniques (iterated balayage) for interaction-energy minimizers—methodologically adjacent to phase-field and (\Gamma)-convergence approaches used around minimal surfaces.

Nonlocal and (L^\infty) Variational Problems

  • An (L^\infty)-variational problem involving the Fractional Laplacian by Simone Carano, Roger Moser

Commentary: Existence/uniqueness of absolute minimizers of (E_\infty(u)=\|(-\Delta)^s u\|_{L^\infty}) with Dirichlet data and the associated Euler–Lagrange characterization. Techniques in supremal functionals and nonlocal operators can inform regularity/limit analysis in variational PDEs.

Prescribed Scalar Curvature and Concentration Phenomena

  • Infinitely many solutions for the prescribed scalar curvature problem with volcano-like curvature by Tuoxin Li, Juncheng Wei, Haidong Yang

Commentary: Constructs infinitely many positive solutions on ( \mathbb{R}^n ) under nonradial “volcano-like” (K(x)), with non-degeneracy. The blow-up/gluing machinery parallels techniques in geometric variational problems.

Spectral Geometry and Isoperimetric Inequalities

  • Isoperimetric Bounds for Weighted Steklov Eigenvalues with Radial Weights by Friedemann Brock, Francesco Chiacchio

Commentary: Extends Weinstock-type inequalities to weighted Steklov problems with radial weights via explicit spectra and new weighted isoperimetric inequalities; connects spectral bounds with geometric measure tools.

Calibrated Geometry and Special Lagrangians

  • Generic special Lagrangian moduli spaces of a non-Kähler Calabi--Yau threefold by Benjamin Friedman

Commentary: Using Sard–Smale, shows for a comeagre set of Hermitian metrics the moduli of perturbed special Lagrangians is discrete. While in calibrated geometry, transversality/moduli issues echo min-max and deformation themes in minimal surface theory.
