High Interest Papers

Constant Mean Curvature, Capillarity, and Quantitative Rigidity

These papers advance quantitative stability and rigidity for CMC-type free boundary problems, sharpening how deviations in mean curvature control uniform closeness to model geometries and settling shape uniqueness in capillarity-type regimes—central to your interests in CMC and capillary surfaces. * Optimal quantitative stability estimates for Alexandrov's Soap Bubble Theorem via Gagliardo-Nirenberg-type interpolation inequalities by João Gonçalves da Silva, Giorgio Poggesi * Global rigidity of two-dimensional bubbles by Lukas Niebel

Minimal Surfaces: Existence, Stability, and Singular Models

These works deliver new existence results for closed minimal surfaces with prescribed curvature sign in high-dimensional spheres, rigidity for stable minimal immersions under ambient curvature hypotheses, and new families of area-minimizing cones—directly tied to min-max/GMT and singularity models. * Minimal surfaces with negative curvature in large dimensional spheres by Michele Ancona, François Labourie, Anna Roig-Sanchis, Jérémy Toulisse * Stable Bernstein Problem in certain positively curved manifolds by Xuan Yao * Some configuration results for area-minimizing cones by Yongsheng Zhang

Mean Curvature Flow and Discrete Interface Motion

These papers establish sharp global geometric control for mean curvature flow—uniform bounded intrinsic diameter as (t \nearrow T), (L^1)-bounds for (|A|), weak (L^3) space-time control, and (\mathcal{H}^1)-finite singular set—and rigorously connect lattice interface dynamics to crystalline mean curvature flow via minimizing movements, including pinning phenomena depending on a parameter (\gamma). * The Bounded Diameter Conjecture and Sharp Geometric Estimates for Mean Curvature Flow by Yiqi Huang, Wenshuai Jiang * Crystalline motion of discrete interfaces in the Blume-Emery-Griffiths model by Marco Cicalese, Giuliana Fusco, Giovanni Savaré

Free Boundary Problems: Construction and Regularity

New constructions of free boundary minimal surfaces in products of balls are obtained via extremal mixed Steklov–Neumann eigenvalue problems with symmetry, while the Alt–Caffarelli free boundary sees generic real-analyticity in (n=6) and improved singular set dimension bounds via a careful study of the linearized equation (Harnack and spectral gap). These resonate with min-max/spectral constructions and free boundary regularity techniques used in GMT. * Free boundary minimal surfaces in products of balls by Jaigyoung Choe, Ailana Fraser, Richard Schoen * Linearized equation and generic regularity in the Alt-Caffarelli problem by Xavier Fernández-Real, Hui Yu

Phase Transitions and Variational PDE

These papers strengthen the analytical foundations of phase-field models closely tied to Allen–Cahn/Ginzburg–Landau heuristics: convergence of weak solutions of Cahn–Hilliard with non-degenerate mobility and singular potentials to a single equilibrium in 3D under minimal hypotheses, and a complete description of global minimizers (kinks) for a one-dimensional, forced, periodically modulated model from nematic light–matter interaction. * Convergence to equilibrium of weak solutions to the Cahn--Hilliard equation with non-degenerate mobility and singular potential by Maurizio Grasselli, Andrea Poiatti * Energy minimizers in a periodic phase transition model of light-matter interaction in nematic liquid crystals by Panayotis Smyrnelis, Marcel G. Clerc, Manuel Diaz-Zuniga, Michał Kowalczyk

Variational and PDE Aspects of Fluids

These works touch variational structures and singularity analysis in fluid models: a quantitative classification framework for potentially singular 3D Navier–Stokes solutions near and beyond blow-up times amenable to numerical verification, and geometric obstructions in the helicity isoperimetric problem constraining candidate maximizers among domains of fixed volume. * Quantitative classification of potential Navier-Stokes singularities beyond the blow-up time by Tobias Barker * Non-optimal domains for the helicity maximisation problem by Wadim Gerner

Calculus of Variations: Relaxation and Nonconvex Integrands

Foundational relaxation results for integral functionals (\displaystyle F(u)=\int_{\Omega} f(\nabla u)\,dx) with superlinear, highly discontinuous, possibly unbounded, nonconvex (f), showing absence of Lavrentiev gaps under minimal assumptions—useful background for nonconvex energies arising in geometric variational problems. * Relaxation for highly discontinuous, possibly unbounded, integral functionals by Tommaso Bertin, Giulia Treu

Fully Nonlinear/Calibrated Geometry PDE

Regularity and Liouville-type results for semi-convex viscosity solutions to the special Lagrangian equation at almost negative phase, with sharpness and effective Hessian bounds—techniques adjacent to curvature equations encountered in geometric analysis. * Semi-convex viscosity solutions of the special Lagrangian equation by Connor Mooney, Ravi Shankar

Magnetic Geodesics and Infinite-Dimensional Geometry

Extension of Mañé’s critical value and Hopf–Rinow phenomena to right-invariant magnetic systems on half Lie-groups, yielding Finslerian geodesic behavior above the critical energy—interesting for variational/geodesic flows but peripheral to minimal-surface themes. * The Hopf--Rinow Theorem and Mañé's Critical Value for Magnetic Geodesics on Half Lie-Groups by Levin Maier, Francesco Ruscelli
