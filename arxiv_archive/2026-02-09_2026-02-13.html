Geometric flows (mean curvature flow and variants, including hyperbolic setting and free boundary)

These works develop tools and rigidity for curvature-driven evolutions that are central to min-max, singularity analysis, and comparison-principle techniques. Of particular note: Aleksandrov reflection for level-set flows in hyperbolic space and rigidity/parametrization of ancient free-boundary solutions around unstable free-boundary minimal hypersurfaces.

  • Aleksandrov reflection for Geometric Flows in Hyperbolic Spaces by Theodora Bourni, José M. Espinar, Aakash Mishra
  • Modified mean curvature flow of graphs in Riemannian manifolds by Jocel Faustino Norberto de Oliveira, Jorge Herbert Soares de Lira, Matheus Nunes Soares
  • Ancient solutions to free boundary mean curvature flow by Theodora Bourni, Giada Franz
  • Convergence to pushed fronts and the behavior of level sets in monostable reaction-diffusion equations by Ryo Kiyono

Why it matters: - Hyperbolic-space Aleksandrov reflection in level-set formulation yields starshapedness and exponential convergence to umbilic limits at infinity, aligning with interest in hyperbolic (3)-manifolds and IMCF. - A priori height/gradient/curvature estimates for modified MCF of graphs (with a Killing field) strengthen long-time existence frameworks useful in barrier constructions. - The free-boundary ancient solution rigidity and the index–parameter correspondence clarify the landscape of ancient solutions emanating from unstable free-boundary minimal hypersurfaces, informing stability/instability analyses. - The pushed-front convergence with an effective mean curvature flow with drift connects reaction-diffusion front geometry to motion by mean curvature, supplying a bridge to geometric front laws.

Mean curvature equation: stability, regularity, and Liouville theorems

New inner regularity and sharp interior gradient estimates for solutions to the prescribed mean curvature equation strengthen the analytic toolkit around minimal/CMC graphs, with direct implications for rigidity and uniform ellipticity away from critical sets.

  • Inner regularity and Liouville theorems for stable solutions to the mean curvature equation by Fanheng Xu
  • Improved Interior Gradient Estimates for the Mean Curvature Equation under Nonlinear Assumptions by Fanheng Xu

Why it matters: - Optimal Morrey-type regularity for (\nabla u) of stable solutions and Liouville results under quantified decay sharpen stability-based rigidity in dimensions (2\le n\le 10), echoing classical minimal surface theory. - Oscillation-based gradient bounds for (\mathrm{div}!\left(\nabla u/\sqrt{1+|\nabla u|^2}\right)=f(\nabla u)) (including (f=0) and IMCF regularizations) yield uniform ellipticity away from the critical set and affine rigidity for global solutions with at-most-linear growth.

Phase-field and sharp-interface limits (Allen–Cahn and variants)

A matrix-valued Allen–Cahn model is shown to converge to a geometric sharp-interface limit via modulated energy, expanding phase-field approximations beyond scalar multi-well potentials and avoiding spectral analysis of linearized operators.

  • The sharp interface limit of the matrix-valued Allen-Cahn equation by Xingyu Wang

Why it matters: - Provides a robust weak-convergence route to interface evolution and constructs weak solutions to the limiting harmonic heat flow with mixed boundary conditions—relevant to phase-field approaches approximating minimal surfaces and curvature-driven flows.

Variational approximations to Plateau/minimal surfaces

Existence and Hölder regularity for minimizers of a Plateau-approximation functional generalize Steiner-type approximations to higher-dimensional surfaces, contributing to computational and variational approaches to minimal surfaces.

  • Existence and Regularity of Minimizers for a Plateau Approximation Problem by Eve Machefert

Why it matters: - Advances controlled approximations to Plateau’s problem in higher codimension, with quantitative regularity, complementing min-max and phase-field schemes for constructing minimal surfaces.

Harmonic map flows (self-expanders and non-uniqueness)

Construction of nontrivial self-expanding solutions emanating from non-energy-minimizing (0)-homogeneous data into hemispheres, with infinitely many weak solutions satisfying monotonicity.

  • Existence of expanding harmonic map flows to hemispheres by Xuanyu Li

Why it matters: - Establishes existence/non-uniqueness in an expanding regime with parabolic monotonicity, echoing themes in geometric flows (e.g., self-expanders in MCF) and providing testbeds for blow-up/continuation scenarios.

Why it matters: - Morrey-type iteration and Hölder-to-(C^{1,\alpha}) upgrades in (\mathbb{H}^n) inform regularity questions for nonlocal energies and variational problems on sub-Riemannian manifolds, relevant to non-Euclidean GMT contexts.
