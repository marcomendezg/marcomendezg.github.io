High Interest Papers

Minimal hypersurfaces and Calabi–Yau phenomena

  • On the Calabi-Yau Conjectures for Minimal Hypersurfaces in Higher Dimensions by Shrey Aryan, Alexander McWeeney

Relevance: Directly targets the Calabi–Yau unboundedness/properness questions for complete minimal hypersurfaces ( \Sigma^n\subset \mathbb{R}^{n+1} ) with ( n\ge 3 ). The chord-arc estimate links intrinsic and extrinsic geometries under curvature control, and the gluing construction yields the first improperly embedded examples in higher dimensions, sharpening our understanding of min–max outputs and ambient geometry constraints.

Mean curvature flow singularities (Lagrangian)

  • Generic neck pinch singularities along 2D Lagrangian mean curvature flow by Gábor Székelyhidi

Relevance: Establishes a robust notion of nondegenerate neck pinches for Lagrangian MCF of surfaces in Calabi–Yau 2-folds, proves their genericity and stability, and excludes nondegenerate “teardrop” singularities in the embedded case. This advances the singularity model classification in MCF and connects to the analyst’s toolkit for understanding flow-through-singularities in variational settings.

Curvature pinching and rigidity of submanifolds

  • Geometric and topological rigidity of pinched submanifolds in Riemannian manifolds by Theodoros Vlachos

Relevance: Sharp rigidity under extrinsic pinching involving the second fundamental form and mean curvature (H), yielding nonnegative isotropic curvature and strong topological conclusions. In higher dimensions the Bochner technique and isotropic curvature drive the analysis; in dimension (4) the argument uses specific 4D structures. This is tightly connected to sphere theorems and constraints on minimal/CMC geometry via extrinsic curvature bounds.

Small normal curvature immersions and sphere theorems

  • Immersions with small normal curvature by Otis Chodosh, Chao Li

Relevance: Resolves Gromov’s problem for the minimal possible normal curvature of ( S^n\times S^1 ) in the unit ball, proves a differentiable sphere theorem, and establishes existence of minimizers. The control of normal curvature offers a complementary variational/geometric constraint to mean curvature, with implications for constructing and classifying near-minimal configurations under extrinsic curvature budgets.

CMC hypersurfaces in product spaces

  • On a class of hypersurfaces of a product of two space forms by Arnando Carvalho, Ruy Tojeiro

Relevance: An explicit construction of hypersurfaces in ( \mathbb{Q}{c_1}^{k} \times \mathbb{Q}{c_2}^{n-k+1} ) with flat normal bundle, reducing CMC in class ( \mathcal A ) to isoparametric families and an ODE, and a classification with constant product angle. This gives a structured pipeline for building/parametrizing CMC examples, useful for testing conjectures and for min–max targets in non-spaceform ambients.

Weingarten surfaces and elliptic curvature equations

  • Asymptotic behavior at infinity of Weingarten surfaces by Aires E. M. Barbieri, José A. Gálvez, Yuanyuan Lian, Kai Zhang

Relevance: Derives precise end expansions and a maximum principle at infinity for uniformly elliptic Weingarten surfaces with finite total curvature in ( \mathbb{R}^3 ), and solves a Dirichlet problem on strictly convex domains. The techniques parallel minimal surface end analysis and strengthen control for general prescribed curvature relations ( W(\kappa_1,\kappa_2)=0 ).

Lorentzian minimal/CMC surfaces via harmonic maps

  • Framed null curves and timelike surfaces via Lorentzian harmonic maps into de-Sitter 2-space by Shintaro Akamine, Hirotaka Kiyohara
  • Singularities on timelike minimal surfaces in Lorentzian Heisenberg group by Shintaro Akamine, Hirotaka Kiyohara

Relevance: Constructs timelike CMC (H) surfaces in ( \mathbb{L}^3 ) and timelike minimal surfaces in ( \operatorname{Nil}_3(H) ) via Lorentzian harmonic maps to ( \mathbb{S}^{2}_1 ), and classifies typical singularities (e.g., cuspidal edges, swallowtails). While in Lorentzian settings, the harmonic map/DPW-style representation and singularity taxonomy mirror tools familiar from minimal/CMC surface theory.

Medium Interest Papers

Nonlocal harmonic maps and flows

  • Fine regularity of fractional harmonic maps and applications by Kyeongbae Kim, Simon Nowak, Yannick Sire

Relevance: Develops small-energy regularity and higher differentiability for fractional harmonic maps (including free boundary cases) without relying on a monotonicity formula. The nonlocal techniques resonate with fractional/phase-transition approximations to interfaces and could inform regularity arguments for nonlocal min–max surrogates.

Prescribed curvature via conformal metrics

  • Conformal Metrics on the Disk with Prescribed Negative Gaussian Curvature and Boundary Geodesic Curvature by Rafael López-Soriano, Francisco J. Reyes-Sánchez, David Ruiz

Relevance: Variational existence and compactness (via refined blow-up with bounded Morse index) for prescribing (K<0) in the disk with boundary geodesic curvature. The bubbling/Morse index analysis parallels techniques in semilinear elliptic PDEs such as Allen–Cahn, with potential methodological crossovers.

Variational energies for maps

  • An Inequality Comparing the Dirichlet Energy and the Bienergy of Maps Between Riemannian Manifolds by Sergey Stepanov, Irina Tsyganok

Relevance: Establishes ( E_2(f)\ge \operatorname{Ric}_{\min}\, E_1(f) ) for maps into nonpositively curved targets, with rigidity at equality. Connects first- and second-order variational problems (harmonic vs biharmonic), conceptually adjacent to Willmore-type energies.

Discrete differential geometry and integrable transforms

  • Constant curvature rotational nets and periodic Bäcklund transforms by Thomas Raujouan, Wayne Rossman, Naoya Suda

Relevance: Explicit constructions of discrete negative curvature surfaces and periodic Bäcklund transforms, providing integrable-structure models that may inform computational approaches to constant curvature and CMC discretizations.

Rigidity in semi-Riemannian submanifold geometry

  • On the Geometry of Complete Spacelike LW-Submanifolds in Locally Symmetric Semi-Riemannian Spaces by Jogli G. S. Araújo, Weiller F. C. Barboza

Relevance: Simons-type identities with the Cheng–Yau operator ( \mathcal L ) yield sharp inequalities and rigidity (totally umbilical/isoparametric) for spacelike linear Weingarten submanifolds under curvature constraints. Technique-rich and thematically close to rigidity for CMC/Weingarten classes.

Regularity for variational problems with interfaces

  • Morrey estimates for the gradient in non-linear variational transmission problems by Luca Esposito, Lorenzo Lamberti

Relevance: Proves local Morrey-space bounds ( \nabla u\in L^{2,\lambda} ) for minimizers of nonlinear energies across a codimension-one interface, via quantitative decay near the interface. The decay/flattening scheme is methodologically relevant to multi-phase and interfacial limits of variational models like ( \int_\Omega F(\nabla u)\,dx ).
