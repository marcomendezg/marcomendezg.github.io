Mean Curvature Flow and Hyperbolic 3-Manifolds

These results address self-similar MCF solitons and minimal surfaces in (\mathbb{H}^3) by imposing a translation-surface ansatz in the half-space model. Capillarity and Prescribed Mean Curvature

  • Rate independent capillary motion on a narrow Wilhelmy plate by Carson Collins, William M Feldman

A rate-independent energetic framework for contact-line dynamics with capillary contact-angle conditions; comparison principles for the prescribed mean curvature equation and barrier constructions yield stability as the domain aspect ratio diverges. The limit removes a volume constraint, linking to settings where monotonicity/uniqueness are known. This dovetails with geometric measure theory and capillary CMC surfaces at walls, including regularity at the contact line.

Nonlocal Perimeter Gradient Flows

Existence and (1/2)-Hölder-in-time estimates for flat flows driven by generalized nonlocal perimeters (fractional, Riesz-type, Minkowski pre-content), plus semigroup property and attraction to halfspaces. This extends gradient-flow machinery in nonlocal geometric measure theory, relevant to nonlocal minimal surfaces and curvature-driven dynamics beyond the local mean curvature flow.

Multiplicity of Constant Mean Curvature Hypersurfaces

  • Existence of multiple constant mean curvature hypersurfaces for varying Riemannian metrics by Xiaoxiang Jiao, Wenduo Zou

For (3\le n+1\le 7) and any fixed (c>0), small metric perturbations increase the number of closed (c)-CMC hypersurfaces while preserving existing ones, with an explicit upper bound on (\|g-h\|_{L^{\frac{n+1}{2}}}). This aligns with min-max/multiplicity themes for CMC (and minimal) hypersurfaces, suggesting perturbative routes to produce more CMC geometries in the regular dimension range.
