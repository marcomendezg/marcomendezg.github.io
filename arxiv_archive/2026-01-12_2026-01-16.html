Anisotropic GMT and Inequalities

  • Michael-Simon inequality for anisotropic energies close to the area via multilinear Kakeya-type bounds by Guido De Philippis, Alessandro Pigati
  • A monotonicity formula for anisotropic minimal hypersurfaces by Doanh Pham

Commentary: Tools analogous to the classical Michael–Simon inequality and monotonicity are established for anisotropic integrands near area. De Philippis–Pigati prove a Michael–Simon inequality for convex (F) close to (1) in (C^1) for (k=2) in (\mathbb{R}^3), cover (\ell^p) norms, and relate validity to compactness of rectifiable varifolds—crucial for regularity/min-max in anisotropic settings where a standard monotonicity fails. Pham provides a monotonicity formula under a sign assumption on the Minkowski norm, yielding the key GMT staple for anisotropic minimal hypersurfaces.

Min-Max Theory and Multiplicity

  • Multiplicity one for equivariant min-max theory in prescribed homology classes by Tongrui Wang

Commentary: Proves a generic multiplicity-one theorem in the equivariant min–max setting and, generically, infinitely many (G)-invariant minimal hypersurfaces in a fixed (G)-homology class, plus a prescribed mean curvature theory with (G)-index bounds. This directly advances min–max multiplicity control and index estimates in symmetric contexts central to your work.

Mean Curvature Flow: Ancient Solutions and Translators

  • Classification of ancient ovals in higher dimensional mean curvature flow by Beomjun Choi, Wenkui Du, Ziyi Zhao
  • Half-space theorems for translating solitons of the r-mean curvature flow by Hilário Alencar, G. Pacelli Bessa, Gregório Silva Neto

Commentary: Choi–Du–Zhao classify compact noncollapsed ancient ovals with specified asymptotics in all dimensions, identifying the moduli and giving an alternative spectral parametrization route consistent with the Bamler–Lai framework—key for the landscape of ancient MCF solutions. Alencar–Bessa–Silva Neto establish half-space-type nonexistence and confinement results for translating solitons of the (r)-MCF, sharpening geometric barriers relevant to translator rigidity.

CMC and Quantitative Rigidity

  • Linear Quantitative Rigidity for Almost-CMC Surfaces by Yuchen Bi, Jie Zhou

Commentary: Under a sub-two-sphere Willmore bound and small (\|H - \bar H\|_{L^2}), obtain linear control of the (W^{2,2}) parametrization distance and the (L^\infty) conformal factor to a round sphere, with an extension to integral varifolds of unit density. This strengthens quantitative stability near spheres in the CMC/Willmore regime and complements variational perturbation approaches.

Nonlocal Isoperimetric Energies and BV on Manifolds

  • A Riemannian Autocorrelation Function and its Application to Non-Local Isoperimetric Energies by Michael Bleher, Denis Brazke, Sebastian Nill

Commentary: Introduces a Riemannian autocorrelation (c_\Omega) linking BV on manifolds to geodesic difference quotients, with (\mathrm{Lip}(c_\Omega)) controlling perimeter. On (S^n), rewrites a nonlocal energy (E_{\gamma,\varepsilon}) with kernel ((1-\varepsilon^2\Delta)^{-1}) in terms of (c_\Omega) and proves (\Gamma)-convergence as (\varepsilon\to0). This provides a clean pathway from nonlocal to local perimeter functionals relevant to phase-transition approximations of interfaces.

Kakeya-Type Methods for Geodesics

  • On unions of geodesics and projections of invariant sets by Longhui Li

Commentary: Extends multilinear Kakeya/Bourgain–Guth techniques to geodesic flow on (SM), giving lower bounds on (\dim_{\mathcal H}\pi(E)) from (\dim_{\mathcal H}E) for invariant sets. These projection/union-of-geodesics bounds are useful for geometric measure problems on manifolds and may inform quantitative transversality in min–max sweepouts.

Bernstein-Type and Affine Differential Geometry

  • Three Bernstein type theorems for hypersurfaces with zero Gaussian curvature by Slawomir Dinew, Mengru Guo, Heming Jiao
  • Calabi affine maximal surfaces and centroaffine Bernstein problems by Yalin Sun, Cheng Xing, Ruiwei Xu

Commentary: Dinew–Guo–Jiao prove Bernstein-type rigidity for entire convex graphs with zero Gaussian curvature in Euclidean and Minkowski settings under natural decay assumptions on mean curvature. Sun–Xing–Xu solve all five centroaffine Bernstein problems posed by Li–Li–Simon (2004) and construct new complete Calabi affine maximal/centroaffine extremal examples. Both deepen the landscape of Bernstein phenomena and variational affine geometry allied to minimal/CMC themes.

Minimal Surfaces in Spheres and Gap Phenomena

  • Lu's conjecture for minimal surfaces by Weiran Ding, Jianquan Ge, Fagui Li, Xize Yang

Commentary: Confirms Lu’s conjecture for minimal 2-spheres in (\mathbb{S}^{n+q}) (and more generally under mild normal scalar curvature conditions), sharpening Simons/Yau–Shen–Li–Li-type gap theorems. This advances spectral/curvature gap rigidity for minimal submanifolds in spheres.

Bochner Identities and Minimal Slicings

  • A universal Bochner formula for scalar curvature by Sven Hirsch

Commentary: Presents a “universal” Bochner formula encompassing the stability inequality for minimal slicings, a Schrödinger–Lichnerowicz-type identity, and higher-dimensional level-set identities. Such unified identities can streamline curvature estimates and stability analyses in variational settings.

Nonlocal Obstacle Problems: Regularity Tools

  • Optimality in nonlocal time-dependent obstacle problems by Ioannis Athanasopoulos, Luis Caffarelli, Emmanouil Milakis

Commentary: Establishes optimal regularity for the temporal derivative and continuity criteria via quasiconvexity for nonlocal, time-dependent obstacle problems. These techniques are directly transferable to nonlocal phase-transition/obstacle models approximating interfaces and free boundaries.

Medium Interest Papers

Scalar Curvature, Surgery, and Total Mean Curvature

  • Quantitative surgery and total mean curvature by Georg Frenck, Bernhard Hanke, Sven Hirsch

Commentary: Develops a quantitative version of Gromov–Lawson/Lawson–Michelsohn surgery and proves a conjecture of Gromov on the total mean curvature of fill-ins. While primarily scalar-curvature/topology focused, the quantitative mean curvature control may inspire constraints relevant to fill-in problems and geometric inequalities.

Variational Models for Nematic Films

  • Existence and uniqueness of minimizers for axisymmetric nematic films by Giulia Bevilacqua, Chiara Lonati, Luca Lussardi, Alfredo Marzocchi

Commentary: Reduces a surface energy with surface tension and nematic elasticity on axisymmetric spanning surfaces to a 1D variational problem, proving existence/uniqueness and characterizing minimizers, with numerics. Relevant as an applied variational surface model with geometric constraints.

Nonlocal Interfaces and Γ-Convergence to Anisotropic Perimeter

  • Non-local planelike minimizers and Γ-convergence of periodic energies to a local anisotropic perimeter by Serena Dipierro, Matteo Novaga, Enrico Valdinoci, Riccardo Villa

Commentary: Shows existence of planelike minimizers for nonlocal energies with periodic forcing and (\Gamma)-convergence of rescaled energies to a local anisotropic perimeter determined by a stable norm. Conceptually close to phase-transition approximations of anisotropic minimal surfaces, though the focus is homogenization rather than core min–max/flow.
