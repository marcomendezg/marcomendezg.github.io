Mean Curvature Flow: Translators and Brakke flows

Briefly: Foundational analytic and Fredholm theory for the linearized translator operator in (\mathbb{R}^4) (the first non-Bernstein dimension) and quantitative consistency results for Brakke-type discretizations. These tools clarify the moduli of noncollapsed translators and strengthen links between weak flows and classical MCF, directly relevant for singularity analysis and varifold approaches.

  • A gradient estimate for the linearized translator equation by Kyeongsu Choi, Robert Haslhofer, Or Hershkovits
  • The linearized translator equation and applications by Kyeongsu Choi, Robert Haslhofer, Or Hershkovits
  • On the avoidance principle for codimension 1 space-time Brakke flows by Abdelmouksit Sagueni
  • Integral Brakke approximate equality for an approximate mean curvature flow by Abdelmouksit Sagueni

Min-max and GMT methods in nonlocal/physical variational models

Briefly: A min-max construction for Gamow’s liquid drop functional (\mathcal{E}) yields non-minimizing volume-constrained critical points, importing “pull-tight” and multiplicity control à la minimal surfaces. The calibrated Reifenberg theorem with holes enhances rectifiability/measure bounds of calibrated sets, feeding directly into regularity frameworks for area-minimizers and related varifolds.

  • Mountain Pass Critical Points of the Liquid Drop Model by Gregory R. Chambers, Jared Marx-Kuo
  • Calibrated Reifenberg With Holes by Susanna Bertolini, Alessandro Preti, Daniele Valtorta

Phase transitions and Allen–Cahn/Ginzburg–Landau

Briefly: Rigidity and classification for vector-valued Allen–Cahn minimizers in (\mathbb{R}^2) with three wells when the induced degenerate metric violates the strict triangle inequality, proving one-dimensionality and excluding three-well heteroclinics. Sharp convergence criteria for Ginzburg–Landau with boundary degree (0): if (\frac12\int_\Omega |\nabla u_\varepsilon|^2 dx \le C_1 \le \frac12\int_\Omega |\nabla u_0|^2 dx), then (u_\varepsilon \to u_0) in (H^1(\Omega)); conversely energy gaps prevent (|u_\varepsilon|\to 1) uniformly. Also, a (\Gamma)-limit and incompressible penalty flows for interfacial energies provide a PDE-to-sharp-interface bridge relevant to Allen–Cahn-type approximations.

  • Minimizing solutions of degenerate Allen-Cahn equations with three wells in (\mathbb{R}^2) by Lia Bronsard, Étienne Sandier, Peter Sternberg
  • On the Convergence of Solutions for the Ginzburg-Landau Equation and System by Rejeb Hadiji, Jongmin Han
  • Gradient Flows of Interfacial Energies: Curvature Agents and Incompressibility by Keith Promislow, Truong Vu, Brian Wetton

Free boundary problems and links to minimal surfaces

Briefly: New homogeneous cones for the one-phase problem in (\mathbb{R}^3) with link domains on (\mathbb{S}^2) with (k) boundary components for large (k), answering Jerison–Kamburov and refuting a conjecture of Souam. The construction exploits a novel connection to minimal surface geometry, resonating with min-max heuristics and symmetry-breaking in free boundary settings.

  • New Homogeneous Solutions for the One-Phase Free Boundary Problem by Coleman Hines, James Kolesar, Peter McGrath

Constant mean curvature and PMC surfaces in curved ambient manifolds

Briefly: In finite-volume hyperbolic (3)-manifolds, area bounds for embedded CMC surfaces with (H\ge 1) in terms of (H) and genus yield compactness; in particular Bryant surfaces ((H=1)) have area (\lesssim) genus—key for compactness/moduli in hyperbolic (3)-geometry. A classification of PMC spheres in (\mathbb{S}^2\times\mathbb{H}^2) when the associated holomorphic quadratic differential vanishes adds to the catalog of special mean curvature geometries.

  • Area bounds for constant mean curvature surfaces in hyperbolic 3-manifolds by Ruojing Jiang
  • Spheres with parallel mean curvature in (\mathbb{S}^2 \times \mathbb{H}^2) by Giel Stas, Joeri Van der Veken

Critical elliptic PDE and bubbling analysis

Briefly: Multiscale pointwise control for high-order critical equations (P_\alpha u_\alpha = |u_\alpha|^{2^*-2}u_\alpha) on (M) with energy bounds: uniform domination by a finite sum of explicit bubbles plus a smooth background (u_\infty). This sharp bubble decomposition is directly aligned with concentration-compactness and blow-up tools used in min-max theory.

  • Sharp multiscale control for high order nonlinear equations by Frédéric Robert

Capillary surfaces and isoperimetric inequalities

Briefly: A sharp comparison for capillary energy with contact angle (\theta\in(0,\pi)) outside arbitrary convex supports: any such surface has larger energy than the spherical cap in the half-space with the same enclosed volume and (\theta), with rigidity. This generalizes the Choe–Ghomi–Ritoré case (\theta=\frac{\pi}{2}) and is relevant to capillarity variational problems.

  • The isoperimetric inequality for the capillary energy outside convex sets by N. Fusco, V. Julin, M. Morini, A. Pratelli
