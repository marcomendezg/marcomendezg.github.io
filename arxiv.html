Advances in Mean Curvature Flow and Brakke Theory

  • Singularities of Curve Shortening Flow with Convex Projections by Qi Sun
  • Local umbilic, convexity and cylindrical estimates for fully nonlinear curvature flows by Mat Langford, James McCoy
  • Mean curvature flow converging to an minimizing cone and its Hardt-Simon foliation by Jiuzhou Huang
  • $L^2$ normal velocity implies strong solution for graphical Brakke flows by Kotaro Motegi

These works sharpen our understanding of singularity formation, pinching, and regularity in curvature flows. Sun proves Type I singularities and asymptotic circularity for closed curves with a convex planar projection in (\mathbb{R}^n), and an analog of Huisken’s “round point” conjecture via a codimension-(2) perturbation. Langford–McCoy localize umbilic/convexity/cylindrical estimates for fully nonlinear, one-homogeneous speeds, extending core MCF technology to broader flows. Huang constructs MCFs that, under Type I rescaling, converge to a strictly stable area-minimizing cone (\mathcal{C}) and, under Type II rescaling, to the Hardt–Simon foliation—without symmetry assumptions—linking flow asymptotics to GMT models of singularities. Motegi shows that graphical Brakke flows with (L^2) normal velocity are strong solutions (and classical under suitable forcing), bridging varifold notions with PDE regularity in (\mathbb{R}^{n+1}). Together these reinforce tools crucial for min–max limits and singularity analysis.

Minimal Surfaces: Index, Deformations, and Global Classification

  • Note on energy index and first eigenvalue of minimal surfaces in spheres by Matilde Gianocca
  • Neutral Deformation Modes of Minimal Surfaces by Ande M. Sonnet, Epifanio G. Virga
  • Minimal Surfaces of Finite Genus: Classification, Dynamics and Laminations by Joaquín Pérez

Gianocca clarifies the relationship between the first Laplacian eigenvalue (\lambda_1(\Sigma)) and the energy index (\mathrm{ind}_E(\Sigma)) for minimal surfaces in (S^n), proving that if (\lambda_1(\Sigma) < \frac{n-2}{2n}) there exists a direction (orthogonal to Möbius fields) of negative second variation—useful for stability and index estimates in min–max constructions. Sonnet–Virga characterize “neutral” deformation modes of minimal surfaces, establishing a hierarchy among stretching/drilling/bending energies and showing all isometries are globally neutral, giving a clean energy-based lens on infinitesimal moduli versus rigidity. Pérez’s ICM-style survey consolidates three decades of structural theory—lamination techniques, dynamics, topology/index bounds, and moduli—for properly embedded minimal surfaces in (\mathbb{R}^3), providing a roadmap and sharpened tools directly aligned with variational and GMT approaches.

Area-Minimizing Cones and Isoparametric Geometry

  • On FKM isoparametric hypersurfaces in (\mathbb{S}^n \times \mathbb{S}^n) and new area-minimizing cones by Hongbin Cui

Cui leverages a refined FKM isoparametric foliation on (\mathbb{S}^n(a)\times \mathbb{S}^n(b)\subset \mathbb{S}^{2n+1}) to produce many new area-minimizing cones (notably codimension-2 cones in (\mathbb{R}^{2n+2}) for (n\ge 63)) via Lawlor’s curvature criterion. This broadens the catalog of stable singular models relevant to min–max limits and to blow-up analysis in mean curvature flow and Allen–Cahn interfaces.

Constant Principal Curvature and Prescribed Curvature Geometry

  • Families of surfaces with constant ratio of principal curvatures and Plateau's problem by Mikhail Skopenkov, Khusrav Yorov
  • Surfaces and Hypersurfaces with Prescribed Radial Mean Curvature by Marcelo Lopes Ferro, Armando M. V. Corro
  • On Hopf hypersurfaces of the complex hyperbolic quadric with constant principal curvatures by Haizhong Li, Hiroshi Tamaru, Zeke Yao

Skopenkov–Yorov construct families of CRPC surfaces (constant ratio of principal curvatures) passing through a given minimal surface without flat points and address Plateau-type problems in this setting—an analytically rich generalization of minimal surfaces. Ferro–Corro give local classifications for surfaces/hypersurfaces with prescribed radial mean curvature, including a class with vanishing second mean curvature and constructive schemes across dimensions. Li–Tamaru–Yao classify Hopf hypersurfaces in the complex hyperbolic quadric (Q^{m*}) with a small number of constant principal curvatures, refining the landscape of homogeneous curvature models that often appear as comparison or barrier geometries in variational problems.

Phase-Field, Allen–Cahn, and Anisotropic Interface Energies

  • An Allen-Cahn tumor growth model with temperature by Stefania Gatti, Erica Ipocoana, Alain Miranville
  • Curvature penalization of strongly anisotropic interfaces models and their phase-field approximation by Jean-François Babadjian, Blanche Buet, Michael Goldman
  • Non-spherical minimizers in the generalized liquid drop model for Yukawa and truncated Coulomb potentials by Lia Bronsard, Benoît Merlet, Marc Pegon

These papers tackle diffuse-interface and sharp-interface energies central to phase transition models approximating minimal surfaces. Gatti–Ipocoana–Miranville analyze a non-isothermal Allen–Cahn/Ginzburg–Landau system, proving global well-posedness—useful for coupled gradient-flow limits and numerical min–max approximations. Babadjian–Buet–Goldman show that adding a Willmore-type curvature penalization restores lower semicontinuity for strongly anisotropic surface tensions and establish (\Gamma)-convergence for anisotropic nonconvex perimeter and Mumford–Shah-type energies, accommodating singular structures like crack-tips and junctions. Bronsard–Merlet–Pegon demonstrate non-spherical minimizers for screened interactions (Yukawa/truncated Coulomb), via energy-per-mass comparisons of balls vs cylinders—informative for understanding when interface energies depart from the classical spherical minimizer paradigm.

Extremal Nonlocal Variational Problems

  • An (L^\infty)-variational problem involving the Fractional Laplacian by Simone Carano, Roger Moser

Carano–Moser study the supremal functional (E_\infty(u)=\|(-\Delta)^s u\|{L^\infty(\mathbb{R}^n)}) with exterior Dirichlet data, proving existence/uniqueness of absolute minimizers and identifying a limiting PDE [ (-\Delta)^s u\infty = E_\infty(u_\infty)\,\mathrm{sgn}\,f_\infty \quad \text{in }\Omega, ] with (f_\infty) linked to (s)-harmonic measure. The methods enrich the toolbox for extremal, nonlocal variational problems appearing in phase-field limits and nonlocal minimal surfaces.

Hyperbolic 3-Manifolds and Rigidity

  • Marked Poincaré rigidity near hyperbolic metrics and injectivity of the Lichnerowicz Laplacian in dimension 3 by Karen Butt, Alena Erchenko, Tristan Humbert, Thibault Lefeuvre, Amie Wilkinson

Introducing the marked Poincaré determinant (MPD), the authors prove that in dimension (3), metrics (g) near a hyperbolic (g_0) with matching MPD are homothetic, and they establish injectivity of the Lichnerowicz Laplacian on trace-free divergence-free tensors. This rigidity sharpens the analytic backdrop for studying minimal/CMC surfaces in hyperbolic 3-manifolds, where spectral properties of geometric operators control deformation theory and stability.

Isoperimetric and Spectral Inequalities

  • Isoperimetric Bounds for Weighted Steklov Eigenvalues with Radial Weights by Friedemann Brock, Francesco Chiacchio

Extending Weinstock-type results, the authors derive sharp isoperimetric inequalities for weighted Steklov eigenvalues with radial weights (w,v), including cases (w(x)=|x|^\alpha), (v(x)=|x|^{\beta-\alpha}) and (w(x)=W(|x|)) with (W) non-decreasing and log-convex. The new weighted inequalities and explicit radial spectra provide flexible comparison principles that can interface with capillarity, free boundary, and shape optimization problems related to mean curvature and perimeter functionals.
