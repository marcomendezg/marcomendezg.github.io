Mean curvature flow, Allen–Cahn approximations, and stochastic/weak solutions

  • Existence of martingale solutions for a stochastic weighted mean curvature flow of graphs by Qi Yan, Xiang-Dong Li
  • Non-uniqueness in Mean Curvature Flow: Non-canonical solutions via the parabolic Allen--Cahn by J. M. Daniels-Holgate

Why this matters for you: both papers bear directly on weak/variational notions of MCF that you study. Daniels-Holgate constructs non-outermost Brakke motions as limits of the parabolic $\varepsilon$-Allen--Cahn flow, producing the first closed, nontrivial integral Brakke motions supported strictly inside fattening regions — a new class of examples relevant to questions about uniqueness, fattening, and $\varepsilon$-approximation schemes. Yan–Li develop existence of martingale solutions for stochastic weighted MCF of graphs (variational SPDE compactness methods) and study small-noise limits; this connects stochastic perturbations of mean curvature flow with probabilistic/variational compactness and could interplay with probabilistic selection principles for weak flows.

Relevant techniques/keywords to note: $\varepsilon$-Allen--Cahn limits to Brakke motions, low-regularity initial data (fractal/Reifenberg sets), variational SPDE compactness, martingale solutions, selection/non-uniqueness phenomena.

Curvature flows: Gauss curvature, inverse (anisotropic) flows, inverse curvature of Legendre curves, Hermitian curvature flow

  • Ancient Gauss Curvature Flows of Bounded Width by Beomjun Choi, Kyeongsu Choi, Dongjun Noh
  • Asymptotic behaviour of the weak inverse anisotropic mean curvature flow by Chaoqun Gao, Yong Wei, Rong Zhou
  • Inverse curvature flow of closed Legendre curves by Takashi Kagaya, Masatomo Takahashi
  • Positive Hermitian curvature flow on 2-step nilpotent Lie groups by Ettore Lo Giudice

Why this matters for you: these works extend existence/asymptotic classifications in several curvature-flow directions you follow. Choi–Choi–Noh construct new ancient solutions (pancake/sausage type) for Gauss/$\alpha$-Gauss curvature flows — examples that illuminate singularity profiles and ancient-solution rigidity. Gao–Wei–Zhou obtain uniform gradient estimates stable as $p\to1$, passing to the weak inverse anisotropic mean curvature flow (IAMCF) and proving asymptotic convergence to Wulff shapes, generalizing Huisken–Ilmanen to anisotropic settings (useful if you consider anisotropic variational structures). Kagaya–Takahashi treat inverse curvature flow for Legendre curves, giving global existence, cusp monotonicity, and classification of scaling limits (self-similar attractors). Lo Giudice analyzes Hermitian curvature flow on 2-step nilpotent Lie groups and long-time behavior / solitons — relevant if you track geometric flows beyond hypersurfaces and are interested in soliton uniqueness up to homothety.

Techniques: gradient estimates uniform in limits ($p\to1$), barrier/asymptotic analysis to Wulff shapes, ancient-solution constructions, classification via rescaling, Cheeger–Gromov limits for long-time behavior.

Geometric measure theory, varifolds, space–time currents, and free-boundary systems

  • The Space-Time Connectivity Theorem for Normal Currents by Paolo Bonicatto, Filip Rindler, Harry Turnbull
  • Young functions on varifolds. Part I. Functional analytic foundations by Hsin-Chuang Chou
  • Vectorial Bernoulli Problems and Free Boundary Systems by Giorgio Tortone, Bozhidar Velichkov
  • Hausdorff dimension of the singular set for Griffith almost-minimizers in the plane by Manuel Friedrich, Camille Labourie, Kerrek Stinson
  • A survey on the optimal partition problem by Roberto Ognibene, Bozhidar Velichkov

Why this matters for you: these papers push the analytic/measure-theoretic machinery you rely on. Bonicatto–Rindler–Turnbull give a space–time variant of connectivity for normal currents that produces a time-coordinate connecting sequence elements to the limit — a potentially useful framework when passing to limits of time-evolving varifolds/Brakke flows or when constructing deformation currents in min–max continuity arguments. Chou develops a functional-analytic foundation for Young functions on varifolds (a multiple-valued / measure-theoretic model) and compactness via associated graph measures — directly relevant to convergence of surfaces together with scalar fields (e.g., phase-field approximations like Allen–Cahn). Tortone–Velichkov and Ognibene–Velichkov survey vectorial Bernoulli/free-boundary systems and optimal partition regularity — central for multi-component variational problems and free-boundary regularity techniques. Friedrich–Labourie–Stinson lower the Hausdorff dimension of Griffith fracture singular sets (uniform rectifiability, ε-regularity), an advance in singular-set size estimates analogous to results for Mumford–Shah and related variational fracture models.

Techniques/keywords: space–time normal currents, weak* convergence, graph-measure compactness for varifolds+functions, ε-regularity and uniform rectifiability, multi-phase free-boundary systems, monotonicity and dimension estimates.

  • Riesz fractional gradient functionals defined on partitions: nonlocal-to-local variational limits by Stefano Almi, Maicol Caponi, Manuel Friedrich, Francesco Solombrino

Why this matters for you: these papers refine the variational foundations that underpin many approximations and min–max constructions. Keywords: relaxation, lower semicontinuity, Lavrentiev gap, $\Gamma$-convergence, nonlocal-to-local limits, variable exponent growth.

Phase-field, gradient-flow PDEs, and long-time dynamics

  • On the Cahn-Hilliard equation with nonlinear diffusion: the non-convex case by Monica Conti, Stefania Gatti, Andrea Giorgini, Giulio Schimperna
  • Derivation of the fourth-order DLSS equation with nonlinear mobility via chemical reactions by Alexander Mielke, André Schlichting, Artur Stephan

Why this matters for you: both papers emphasize gradient-structure, long-time behavior, and justification of higher-order PDEs arising as variational limits. Conti–Gatti–Giorgini–Schimperna remove convexity assumptions for nonlinear-diffusion Cahn–Hilliard, prove uniqueness/smoothing in 2D and obtain convergence to equilibria using a tailored Lojasiewicz–Simon inequality — a method you may use in analyzing phase-field approximations of minimal surfaces. Mielke–et al. derive a fourth-order DLSS equation (and nonlinear-mobility variants) from particle/chemical reaction models and identify the limiting gradient structure via EDP convergence; this clarifies when higher-order gradient flows approximate diffusive/phase-separation dynamics and shows qualitative behaviors (traveling waves with algebraic tails, compact support) that might inform approximation strategies.

Techniques/keywords: Lojasiewicz–Simon inequality, EDP convergence, gradient structures, nonlinear mobility, higher-order PDE limits.
