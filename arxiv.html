Advances in Mean Curvature Flow and Brakke Theory

  • Singularities of Curve Shortening Flow with Convex Projections by Qi Sun
  • Local umbilic, convexity and cylindrical estimates for fully nonlinear curvature flows by Mat Langford, James McCoy
  • Mean curvature flow converging to an minimizing cone and its Hardt-Simon foliation by Jiuzhou Huang
  • $L^2$ normal velocity implies strong solution for graphical Brakke flows by Kotaro Motegi

Commentary: These papers sharpen control of singularities and regularity for curvature flows. Sun proves Type I formation and asymptotic roundness for curve shortening in (\mathbb{R}^n), while Langford–McCoy localize Huisken–Stampacchia to obtain sharp pinching/cylindrical estimates for general one-homogeneous speeds. Huang constructs flows converging (under Type I/II rescalings) to a strictly stable area-minimizing cone and its Hardt–Simon foliation, without symmetry—relevant to tangent flow models of minimal hypersurface singularities. Motegi upgrades graphical Brakke flows with (L^2) normal velocity to strong/classical solutions, bridging varifold (L^2) theory and PDE regularity. For reference, the MCF equation is [ \partial_t x = - H \nu, ] and these results refine how (H) concentrates and regularizes near singular sets.

Minimal Surfaces: Structure, Stability, and Deformations

  • Minimal Surfaces of Finite Genus: Classification, Dynamics and Laminations by Joaquín Pérez
  • Note on energy index and first eigenvalue of minimal surfaces in spheres by Matilde Gianocca
  • Neutral Deformation Modes of Minimal Surfaces by Ande M. Sonnet, Epifanio G. Virga

Commentary: Pérez’s survey synthesizes the Meeks–Pérez–Ros program on classification and lamination techniques, including limit-leaf stability, local removability, and index/topology bounds—central tools for min–max and singularity analysis. Gianocca links the energy index to spectral data on (S^n), giving new negative directions when (\lambda_1(\Sigma)<\tfrac{n-2}{2n}), informing instability thresholds for minimal immersions. Sonnet–Virga classify “neutral” deformation modes between minimal surfaces (isometric/drilling/bending), clarifying the energetic landscape around critical points and offering a clean hierarchy of zero modes.

Area-Minimizing Cones and Isoparametric Geometry

  • On FKM isoparametric hypersurfaces in (\mathbb{S}^n \times \mathbb{S}^n) and new area-minimizing cones by Hongbin Cui

Commentary: Using an isoparametric foliation restricted from FKM to (\mathbb{S}^n\times\mathbb{S}^n), this work applies Lawlor’s curvature criterion to produce many new minimizing cones, notably codimension-2 cones in (\mathbb{R}^{2n+2}) for (n\ge 63). Such stable cones are key models for potential singularities of min–max minimal hypersurfaces and for understanding when regularity fails in high dimensions.

Prescribed Mean Curvature and Curvature-Constrained Classes

  • Families of surfaces with constant ratio of principal curvatures and Plateau's problem by Mikhail Skopenkov, Khusrav Yorov
  • Surfaces and Hypersurfaces with Prescribed Radial Mean Curvature by Marcelo Lopes Ferro, Armando M. V. Corro
  • On Hopf hypersurfaces of the complex hyperbolic quadric with constant principal curvatures by Haizhong Li, Hiroshi Tamaru, Zeke Yao

Commentary: Skopenkov–Yorov construct CRPC surfaces (constant ratio of principal curvatures), a natural generalization of minimal surfaces, and tackle a Plateau-type problem—opening directions for boundary-value problems beyond (H\equiv 0). Ferro–Corro provide local classifications under radial mean curvature prescriptions and a recursive construction scheme, relevant to designing CMC/PMC families. Li–Tamaru–Yao classify Hopf hypersurfaces with few constant principal curvatures in a rank-one symmetric space, enriching the catalog of homogeneous curvature models.

Phase-Field, Allen–Cahn, and Anisotropic Interface Energies

  • An Allen-Cahn tumor growth model with temperature by Stefania Gatti, Erica Ipocoana, Alain Miranville
  • Curvature penalization of strongly anisotropic interfaces models and their phase-field approximation by Jean-François Babadjian, Blanche Buet, Michael Goldman

Commentary: The Allen–Cahn–type tumor model couples order parameter and temperature with well-posedness results—useful for testing diffuse-interface numerics connected to min–max approximations of minimal sets. Babadjian–Buet–Goldman show that adding a Willmore-type curvature penalty restores lower semicontinuity for strongly anisotropic surface energies and prove (\Gamma)-convergence phase-field approximations even with singularities (crack tips/junctions), aligning with interest in curvature-regularized interfacial energies.

Hyperbolic Dynamics and Rigidity Near 3D Hyperbolic Metrics

  • Marked Poincaré rigidity near hyperbolic metrics and injectivity of the Lichnerowicz Laplacian in dimension 3 by Karen Butt, Alena Erchenko, Tristan Humbert, Thibault Lefeuvre, Amie Wilkinson

Commentary: Introducing a marked Poincaré determinant invariant, the authors prove local rigidity near hyperbolic metrics in dimension 3 and show injectivity of the Lichnerowicz Laplacian on TT-tensors. This strengthens the analytic framework around hyperbolic 3-manifolds—fertile ground for embedded minimal surface theory and stability operators analogous to Jacobi fields.

Variational Models, Nonlocal Energies, and Isoperimetric/Spectral Inequalities

  • Isoperimetric Bounds for Weighted Steklov Eigenvalues with Radial Weights by Friedemann Brock, Francesco Chiacchio
  • Non-spherical minimizers in the generalized liquid drop model for Yukawa and truncated Coulomb potentials by Lia Bronsard, Benoît Merlet, Marc Pegon
  • A family of interaction energy minimizers supported on two intervals by Steven B. Damelin, Ruiwen Shu
  • An (L^\infty)-variational problem involving the Fractional Laplacian by Simone Carano, Roger Moser

Commentary: Weighted Steklov inequalities extend classical isoperimetric extremals to radial densities. Bronsard–Merlet–Pegon find non-spherical minimizers under screened interactions, contrasting the Coulombic Gamow picture—relevant as a cautionary analogue for shape optimization. Damelin–Shu’s iterated balayage identifies two-interval supports, and Carano–Moser establish existence/uniqueness of absolute minimizers for (E_\infty(u)=\|(-\Delta)^s u\|_{L^\infty}), adding tools for (L^\infty) nonlocal variational problems.

  • Semi-Global Existence and Trapped Surface Formation for the Einstein-Vlasov System by Nikolaos Athanasiou, Puskar Mondal, Shing-Tung Yau

Commentary: A symmetry-free, large-data formation of trapped surfaces via a refined hierarchy of curvature and kinetic estimates. While GR-focused, the analysis of marginally trapped surfaces and elliptic-hyperbolic couplings may inform geometric PDE techniques around mean curvature and stability in high-codimension settings.
