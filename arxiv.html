Mean Curvature Flow: Non-uniqueness, stochasticity, and phase-field approaches

  • Non-uniqueness in Mean Curvature Flow: Non-canonical solutions via the parabolic Allen--Cahn by J. M. Daniels-Holgate
  • Existence of martingale solutions for a stochastic weighted mean curvature flow of graphs by Qi Yan, Xiang-Dong Li
  • On the Cahn-Hilliard equation with nonlinear diffusion: the non-convex case by Monica Conti, Stefania Gatti, Andrea Giorgini, Giulio Schimperna

Allen–Cahn is used to construct non-outermost weak MCFs supported strictly inside fattening, giving the first closed, non-canonical integral Brakke motions and flows from rough (even fractal) data—highly relevant for phase-field approximations to MCF and singularity selection. The stochastic weighted MCF of graphs establishes martingale solutions and a small-noise limit, informing stability and compactness mechanisms under randomness. The Cahn–Hilliard results (non-convex diffusion) develop a Lojasiewicz–Simon framework and long-time convergence/stability—useful analytic machinery for phase-field models adjacent to $\varepsilon$-Allen--Cahn.

Inverse and Gauss curvature flows (anisotropic and non-smooth regimes)

  • Asymptotic behaviour of the weak inverse anisotropic mean curvature flow by Chaoqun Gao, Yong Wei, Rong Zhou
  • Ancient Gauss Curvature Flows of Bounded Width by Beomjun Choi, Kyeongsu Choi, Dongjun Noh
  • Inverse curvature flow of closed Legendre curves by Takashi Kagaya, Masatomo Takahashi

A uniform gradient estimate for anisotropic $p$-harmonic functions with constants stable as $p\to 1$ yields weak IAMCF well-posedness and asymptotics to Wulff shapes, extending Huisken–Ilmanen’s picture to anisotropy. New ancient compact solutions to Gauss curvature flow and $\alpha$-GCF ($\alpha>\frac{1}{2}$) provide additional models for Type II behavior and “pancake/sausage” geometries relevant to singularity analysis. The inverse curvature flow for $\ell$-convex Legendre curves proves global existence, cusp monotonicity, and convergence to self-similar profiles, offering a finely controlled 1D testbed for inverse-flow techniques.

Geometric Measure Theory: currents, varifolds, and relaxation

  • The Space-Time Connectivity Theorem for Normal Currents by Paolo Bonicatto, Filip Rindler, Harry Turnbull
  • Young functions on varifolds. Part I. Functional analytic foundations by Hsin-Chuang Chou
  • Relaxation of Non-Convex Integral Functionals in the Multidimensional Scalar Case by Tommaso Bertin, Paulin Huguet
  • Relaxation of quasi-convex functionals with variable exponent growth by Giacomo Bertazzoni, Petteri Harjulehto, Peter Hästö, Elvira Zappale
  • Riesz fractional gradient functionals defined on partitions: nonlocal-to-local variational limits by Stefano Almi, Maicol Caponi, Manuel Friedrich, Francesco Solombrino

Space-time connectivity for normal currents constructs a “time-parametrized” connecting current witnessing weak* convergence—conceptually useful for approximating min-max sequences or flows by time-like deformations. The Young-functions-on-varifolds framework develops compactness and convergence for “multi-valued” data on varifolds, a promising new analytic language interfacing with Almgren’s multi-valued theory. The relaxation papers sharpen lower-semicontinuous envelopes for non/ quasi-convex energies (including variable-exponent growth) and $\Gamma$-limits as $s\to 1$ for fractional Riesz-gradient functionals on partitions—core tools for rigorous phase-field and nonlocal-to-local approximations of area/edge energies.

Free boundaries, partitions, and fracture

  • Vectorial Bernoulli Problems and Free Boundary Systems by Giorgio Tortone, Bozhidar Velichkov
  • A survey on the optimal partition problem by Roberto Ognibene, Bozhidar Velichkov
  • Hausdorff dimension of the singular set for Griffith almost-minimizers in the plane by Manuel Friedrich, Camille Labourie, Kerrek Stinson

Two surveys consolidate techniques for vectorial Bernoulli/free-boundary systems and optimal partitions—closely linked to one-phase/multi-phase free-boundary regularity, monotonicity formulas, and blow-up analysis parallel to minimal-surface methods. For Griffith fracture in 2D, uniform rectifiability and $\dim_{\mathcal{H}}(\mathrm{Sing})<1$ are proved, together with higher integrability—strong structural information mirroring Mumford–Shah theory and relevant for understanding singular sets of variational minimizers.

Isoperimetry and minimal surfaces

  • Multi-Bubble Isoperimetric Problems by Emanuel Milman
  • Minimal surfaces and comparison geometry by Otis Chodosh

Milman’s survey synthesizes characterization and stability for multi-bubble minimizers, including soap-film partitions—natural higher-phase analogs of minimal surface problems. Chodosh highlights how minimal surface techniques inform comparison geometry, aligning with min-max constructions, rigidity, and scalar/curvature comparison phenomena.

Hyperbolic 3-manifolds: volume and boundary data

  • Volume functions and boundary data of 3-dimensional hyperbolic manifolds by Jean-Marc Schlenker

A unified account of how renormalized/convex-core volume variations pair with boundary data on convex co-compact manifolds, clarifying duality between “conjugate” boundary variables. The first-variation formulas and uniqueness questions tie into minimal/CMC surface theory in hyperbolic 3-manifolds and variational formulations.

Inequalities for submanifolds and Willmore-type energies

  • Fenchel-Willmore and Sobolev-type inequalities for submanifolds in non-negatively curved manifolds by Meng Ji, Kwok-Kun Kwong

Links between Fenchel–Willmore inequalities and sharp Sobolev/log-Sobolev-type estimates for mean-convex submanifolds yield extensions to non-compact/boundary cases, with topological applications. Useful for secondary interests in Willmore-type energies and for constraints on curvature concentration along flows.

Gradient-flow structures in fourth-order PDE

  • Derivation of the fourth-order DLSS equation with nonlinear mobility via chemical reactions by Alexander Mielke, André Schlichting, Artur Stephan

Derives DLSS and power-mobility variants via reaction networks and EDP convergence, identifying entropy-driven gradient structures and traveling waves with algebraic/compactly supported profiles. Methodologically relevant for understanding energy-dissipation principles and variational limits in higher-order diffusion equations adjacent to geometric flows.
