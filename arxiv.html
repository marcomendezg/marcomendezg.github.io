Advances in Mean Curvature Flow: canonical vs non-canonical solutions, stochasticity, and phase-field limits

  • Non-uniqueness in Mean Curvature Flow: Non-canonical solutions via the parabolic Allen--Cahn by J. M. Daniels-Holgate
  • Existence of martingale solutions for a stochastic weighted mean curvature flow of graphs by Qi Yan, Xiang-Dong Li

Commentary: Daniels-Holgate gives the first construction of closed, non-canonical integral Brakke motions supported strictly inside fattening, obtained as limits of (\varepsilon)-Allen--Cahn flows. This pushes Ilmanen’s program and interacts directly with phase-field approximations of MCF and singularity formation, including flows from rough/fractal initial data. The stochastic graph MCF result establishes martingale solutions for weighted MCF in periodic settings and a small-noise limit, contributing to robustness of MCF under random perturbations—relevant for stability/selection issues in weak flows.

Inverse and anisotropic curvature flows; ancient solutions

  • Asymptotic behaviour of the weak inverse anisotropic mean curvature flow by Chaoqun Gao, Yong Wei, Rong Zhou
  • Ancient Gauss Curvature Flows of Bounded Width by Beomjun Choi, Kyeongsu Choi, Dongjun Noh
  • Inverse curvature flow of closed Legendre curves by Takashi Kagaya, Masatomo Takahashi

Commentary: Gao–Wei–Zhou obtain (p)-harmonic gradient estimates uniform as (p\to 1) to control the weak IAMCF, showing asymptotics to expanding Wulff shapes—an anisotropic analogue of Huisken–Ilmanen’s result. The construction of pancake- and sausage-type ancient solutions for Gauss curvature and (\alpha)-Gauss curvature flows provides new ancient models for singularity analysis. The Legendre inverse curvature flow extends inverse curvature dynamics beyond regular curves, with global existence and classification of asymptotics to self-similar profiles.

Minimal surfaces and GMT: tools and structure

  • Minimal surfaces and comparison geometry by Otis Chodosh
  • The Space-Time Connectivity Theorem for Normal Currents by Paolo Bonicatto, Filip Rindler, Harry Turnbull
  • Young functions on varifolds. Part I. Functional analytic foundations by Hsin-Chuang Chou
  • Relaxation of Non-Convex Integral Functionals in the Multidimensional Scalar Case by Tommaso Bertin, Paulin Huguet

Commentary: Chodosh surveys applications of minimal surfaces in comparison geometry—useful perspective on leveraging min–max and stability to derive global geometric inequalities. The space-time connectivity theorem gives a time-parametrized current connecting weak* convergent normal currents, mirroring homotopies in varifold/Brakke settings and potentially informing convergence of min–max sweepouts. Chou develops a measure-theoretic framework for multiple-valued “functions on varifolds,” promising new analytic tools for fields on varying geometric supports. Bertin–Huguet provide sharp relaxation for non-convex integral energies (E[u]=\int_\Omega f(x,u,\nabla u)\,dx) under Lipschitz boundary data, clarifying lower-semicontinuous envelopes and Lavrentiev-gap avoidance—techniques directly applicable to phase-field and capillarity functionals.

Free-boundary and partition problems: Bernoulli, optimal partitions, and fracture

  • Vectorial Bernoulli Problems and Free Boundary Systems by Giorgio Tortone, Bozhidar Velichkov
  • A survey on the optimal partition problem by Roberto Ognibene, Bozhidar Velichkov
  • Hausdorff dimension of the singular set for Griffith almost-minimizers in the plane by Manuel Friedrich, Camille Labourie, Kerrek Stinson
  • Riesz fractional gradient functionals defined on partitions: nonlocal-to-local variational limits by Stefano Almi, Maicol Caponi, Manuel Friedrich, Francesco Solombrino

Commentary: The two surveys synthesize methods and open problems in vectorial free boundaries and optimal partitions—both tightly linked to multi-phase minimal interfaces and GMT regularity. Friedrich–Labourie–Stinson prove uniform rectifiability and (\dim_{\mathcal H}) of the singular set (<1) for Griffith almost-minimizers, with higher integrability—parallels to Mumford–Shah and minimal-surface singularity bounds. The nonlocal-to-local (\Gamma)-limits for fractional gradient energies on partitions clarify when nonlocal multi-phase energies converge to local interfacial energies, relevant for phase-field approximations of multi-bubble/cluster problems.

Isoperimetric partitions and stability of multi-bubbles

  • Multi-Bubble Isoperimetric Problems by Emanuel Milman

Commentary: A survey of characterization and stability for multi-bubble minimizers (soap-bubble clusters), focusing on quantitative isoperimetry and partition regularity. Closely connected to minimal cluster geometry, calibrations, and stability inequalities that underlie min–max constructions in multi-phase settings.

Hyperbolic 3-manifolds: volume, boundary data, and variational structures

  • Volume functions and boundary data of 3-dimensional hyperbolic manifolds by Jean-Marc Schlenker

Commentary: Reviews rigidity and parameterization by boundary data of convex cores and convex cocompact manifolds, emphasizing renormalized volume and its first variation. The “conjugate variables” viewpoint is variational and resonates with first-variation formulas for area/volume; potentially fruitful for studying minimal/CMC surfaces within hyperbolic manifolds and their moduli.

Variational PDEs and gradient-flow structures adjacent to phase transitions

  • On the Cahn-Hilliard equation with nonlinear diffusion: the non-convex case by Monica Conti, Stefania Gatti, Andrea Giorgini, Giulio Schimperna
  • Derivation of the fourth-order DLSS equation with nonlinear mobility via chemical reactions by Alexander Mielke, André Schlichting, Artur Stephan

Commentary: Conti–Gatti–Giorgini–Schimperna remove convexity of the gradient energy in a Cahn–Hilliard model and, via a tailored Łojasiewicz–Simon inequality, obtain uniqueness/smoothing in 2D and stability in 3D—techniques useful for phase-field models approximating interfaces. Mielke–Schlichting–Stephan derive DLSS-type fourth-order diffusion from reaction networks and identify the limiting gradient-flow structure; contributes to the toolbox for entropy/gradient-flow methods appearing in curvature-driven and phase-field evolutions.

Willmore and Sobolev inequalities on submanifolds

  • Fenchel-Willmore and Sobolev-type inequalities for submanifolds in non-negatively curved manifolds by Meng Ji, Kwok-Kun Kwong

Commentary: Establishes a connection between Fenchel–Willmore-type energies and logarithmic Sobolev inequalities for mean-convex submanifolds in ambient spaces with nonnegative curvature and Euclidean volume growth, yielding sharp Sobolev-type inequalities and topological consequences. These inequalities interface with curvature functionals and can inform compactness/stability in variational problems involving mean curvature.
